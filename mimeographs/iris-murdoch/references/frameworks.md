This document details the practical frameworks Iris Murdoch applied to creative writing, emotional regulation, and conceptual understanding.

## Detailed Pre-Invention of the Novel

**Canonical statement**: Entirely invent the plot and characters before writing the prose.

**Rationale**: To ensure the structure and narrative arc are fully realized, a writer must see the whole picture before beginning the execution phase.
**Steps**:
1. Spend a great deal of time inventing the story.
2. Detail the invention of the plot.
3. Detail the invention of the characters.
4. Visualize the entire book before starting to write the first sentence.

> "I attach great importance to the detailed invention of the plot and the detail advention of the character so that I can see the whole thing before I start to write the first sentence"
*(sources: src_014)*

## Refocusing Attention

**Canonical statement**: Overcome strong negative emotions or unwanted love by acquiring new objects of attention rather than relying on pure willpower.

**Rationale**: Pure willpower cannot simply stop a strong emotion. The mind needs a new focal point to generate different energy.
**Steps**:
1. Recognize that pure willpower cannot simply stop a strong emotion.
2. Acquire new objects of attention.
3. Allow this refocusing to provide energy of a different kind from a different source.

> "Deliberately falling out of love is not a jump of the will, it is the acquiring of new objects of attention and thus of new energies as a result of refocusing"
*(sources: src_006)*

## Joint Attention for Conceptual Understanding

**Canonical statement**: Understand the aesthetic or ethical framework of another person or culture through shared focus on a common object.

**Rationale**: Concepts and values are best understood contextually, by observing how others apply normative language to a shared reality.
**Steps**:
1. Group around a common object of attention (e.g., a work of art).
2. Listen to normative-descriptive talk from an informed other in the presence of the object.
3. Share their context to gradually grasp the beliefs, concerns, and values that structure their concepts.

> "Uses of words by persons grouped round a common object is a central and vital human activity. The art critic can help us if we are in the presence of the same object and if we know something about his scheme of concepts."
*(sources: src_057)*
