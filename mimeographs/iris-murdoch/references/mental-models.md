This document catalogs the mental models Iris Murdoch used to analyze human consciousness, morality, and art.

## The Fat Relentless Ego (The Veil of Fantasy)

**Canonical statement**: The mind tends to fabricate an anxious, preoccupied, and falsifying veil that conceals true reality.

**Rationale**: The ego acts as a distorting filter that assimilates the "other" into a personal fantasy world. This is the primary enemy of both art and morality. Moral progress requires piercing this veil through objectivity and unselfishness to see the world as it truly is.

> "the enemy is the fat relentless ego"
*(sources: src_006, src_017, src_037)*

## Unselfing

**Canonical statement**: The discipline of detaching from the ego's preoccupations to allow reality to appear.

**Rationale**: Unselfing is both an epistemic and moral virtue that clears the field for true attention. It is often occasioned by great art or nature (like watching a hovering kestrel), where the ego disappears, leaving only the object of attention.

> "I would think that people have what I would call selfless experience when – well for instance when they are looking at a great work of art."
*(sources: src_028, src_037, src_057)*

## The Patient Eye of Love

**Canonical statement**: Moral life is a continuous, just, and loving gaze directed upon individual reality.

**Rationale**: Rather than viewing morality as a series of discrete actions or choices, it should be seen as an ongoing mode of observation. It involves observing other people with an undramatic, un-self-centered agnosticism that respects their autonomy and particularity.

> "a just and loving gaze directed upon an individual reality"
*(sources: src_006, src_043, src_057)*

## Imagination vs. Fantasy

**Canonical statement**: Imagination is a creative, truth-seeking power, while fantasy is a private, self-consoling illusion.

**Rationale**: This lens is crucial for evaluating art and thought. Fantasy acts as a menace to creative imagination, resulting in bad art that fails to truthfully reflect the world. True art requires the self-discipline to expel personal fantasy.

> "I think art is very much concerned with removing a veil of fantasy, which normally perhaps wraps one's head as one gazes at the world."
*(sources: src_017)*

## The Divided Self

**Canonical statement**: Human consciousness is split into parts that can judge, disapprove of, or attempt to control one another.

**Rationale**: It is a basic characteristic of human consciousness that we experience internal conflict, demonstrating that one part of the self is judging another. This internal division is the battleground for moral progress and purifying desires.

> "one doesn't have to be philosopher or hold a religious view to think that one is divided, one is a divided person."
*(sources: src_028)*
