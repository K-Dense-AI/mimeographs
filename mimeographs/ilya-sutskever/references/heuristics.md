This document lists Ilya Sutskever's pithy rules of thumb and operational heuristics for AI research and development.

## Bigger is Better
Scaling up neural networks, data, and compute reliably improves performance. If you train a large and deep neural network on a big enough dataset, it must succeed.
> "I had a very strong belief that bigger is better"
(sources: src_015, src_020)

## The 10-Layer Rule
If there is one human in the entire world that can do some task in a fraction of a second, then a 10-layer neural network can do it too.
> "anything that a human being can do in a fraction of a second a big 10 10 layer neural network can do too"
(sources: src_012)

## No Room for Ugliness
Research ideas must be aesthetically pleasing; there is no room for ugliness in fundamental research.
> "Ugliness, there's no room for ugliness."
(sources: src_035)

## Forcing Reasoning
If you want a neural network to reason, you must give it a task where reasoning is the easiest possible way to solve it, because neural networks are lazy optimizers.
> "if you train a neural network on a task which doesn't require reasoning, it's not going to resign."
(sources: src_056)

## Maximize Function Class
Choose the largest possible function class supported by your training set size and compute budget.
> "In practice, it is sensible to choose the largest possible F that can be supported by the size of the training set and the available computation."
(sources: src_057)

## Value Function Equivalence
Anything you can do with a value function, you can do without, just more slowly.
> "I think anything you can do with a value function, you can do without, just more slowly."
(sources: src_035)

## Research First, Monetization Later
Focus purely on the research first, and the business model or monetization strategy will reveal itself later.
> "Right now, we just focus on the research, and then the answer to that question will reveal itself."
(sources: src_027)

## Target Regulation at High Capabilities
Target AI regulations and standards at the very high level of capability (superintelligence), not at current models.
(sources: src_024)
