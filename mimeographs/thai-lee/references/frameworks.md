This document details the structured frameworks Thai Lee uses for life planning, goal setting, and career selection.

## 100-Year Decade-by-Decade Planning
A method for crafting long-term life and career goals by dividing a 100-year lifespan into distinct, singularly focused decades.
1. Assume you will live to be 100 years old to establish a long-term horizon.
2. Create a template for each decade of your life.
3. Assign a singular, overarching focus to each decade (e.g., 20s for learning/preparation, 30s for extreme startup focus, 40s for family integration).
4. Score yourself regularly on whether you are accomplishing the specific goals within your current decade.
> "actually assumed first of all that I would live to a hundred and actually put together a template for each decade"
(sources: src_011, src_012, src_027)

## Holistic SMART Goals
A framework for setting specific objectives within your life plan across health, family, and career.
1. Divide your life into three holistic aspects: personal/family, career/financial, and health.
2. Make goals Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Relatable, and Time-bound.
3. Ensure health goals are prioritized first, as they are the foundation for the other two aspects.
> "setting goals you need to make it smart so specific measurable actionable relatable time-bound"
(sources: src_011)

## Career Selection by Elimination
A method for determining a career path by systematically crossing out professions where personal limitations would prevent top-tier success.
1. Identify personal limitations or weaknesses (e.g., language barriers).
2. Evaluate potential professions and cross out any where those limitations would prevent top-tier success.
3. Commit fully to the remaining viable options.
> "I knew then that the best chance of success for me was to start my own business, because after I x-ed out all the professions I could not be successful in, that's what I was left with."
(sources: src_027)
