This document captures the mental models Thai Lee uses to navigate business challenges and organizational design.

## Salesperson as Client CEO
Viewing each salesperson not as a vendor pushing products, but as the chief executive or president of their client's success. This instills absolute ownership and empowerment over customer relationships, allowing representatives to make their own decisions on how to manage and serve their customers.
(sources: src_012, src_027)

## Disadvantage as Fuel
Reframing personal insecurities or lack of cultural familiarity not as inhibitors, but as the primary source of motivation to out-prepare and out-work others. For example, using insecurity about language skills to craft the most ambitious goals possible.
> "i think low self-esteem was the source of motivation for me to work harder it's an accumulation of directed energy focus and effort and over decades that can be very powerful"
(sources: src_012)

## The Biological Timetable
Viewing family planning as a fixed biological constraint that must be strategically factored into long-term career sequencing, rather than left to chance. She dedicated her 30s entirely to her startup, but explicitly planned to start a family at 39 because of the set timetable for securing a family.
(sources: src_011)

## Health as the Ultimate Prerequisite
Treating physical health not as a separate goal, but as the foundational prerequisite that enables all career and financial goals to exist.
> "Without your health there's really nothing else right that matters in life."
(sources: src_011)

## Right Livelihood
A guiding philosophy for leadership and business that emphasizes staying grounded, human, and respectful, rather than succumbing to the traditional trappings of executive power.
> "a company is only as strong as the respect it shows its people."
(sources: src_007)
