# Core Principles of Martin Heidegger

These principles form the bedrock of Heidegger's philosophy. They are not just theoretical statements, but active rules for interpreting human existence, technology, and truth.

## The Primacy of the Question of Being

**Canonical statement:** The question of what it means to 'be' is the most fundamental inquiry, taking priority over all other scientific or philosophical questions.

**Rationale:** Every positive science operates within a pre-theoretical understanding of its subject matter. Without clarifying the meaning of being itself, any ontological inquiry remains superficial and fails to understand the conditions that make beings accessible to us in the first place.

> "The question of being as such, however, when it is put in a sufficiently formal manner, is the most universal and emptiest, but perhaps also the most concrete question, which a scientific inquiry can ever raise"
*(sources: src_033, src_021, src_030, src_013, src_025)*

## Inseparability of Subject and Object

**Canonical statement:** Human beings are not detached observers of an external world; subject and object are fundamentally inseparable in human existence.

**Rationale:** The traditional separation of subjects and objects is a trap woven into European languages. Reality is actually defined by 'Dasein' (being-there), meaning we are irreducibly situated and embedded in the world.

> "Being-in is something quite different from a mere confrontation, whether by way of observation or by way of action; that is, it is not the Being-present-at-hand- together of a subject and an Object."
*(sources: src_019, src_025, src_013, src_041)*

## Existence Precedes Substance

**Canonical statement:** The essence of human beings lies in their existence, not in fixed, determinative properties or substances.

**Rationale:** Unlike other entities that possess fixed properties, human beings are entities that interpret their own essence. Our essence is open, never fixed once and for all, because our own being is an issue for us.

> "Entities with Dasein's kind of Being cannot ' be conceived in terms of Reality and substantiality; we have expressed this by the thesis that the substance of man is existence."
*(sources: src_036, src_033, src_041)*

## Truth as Unconcealment (Aletheia)

**Canonical statement:** Truth is fundamentally a dynamic process of revealing and concealing, not merely the correctness of a statement matching reality.

**Rationale:** Before a statement can be judged as correct or incorrect, the being it refers to must first emerge from concealment. Furthermore, every act of revealing inherently throws other phenomena into darkness.

> "Truth as un-concealment is not an appendage to being. Truth is inherent in the essence of being."
*(sources: src_021, src_013)*

## The Essence of Technology as Revealing

**Canonical statement:** Technology is not merely a neutral tool or applied science, but a pervasive 'mode of revealing' that frames how entities present themselves to us.

**Rationale:** Technology challenges and orders the world as resources (standing reserve). Modern natural science is actually the application of this technological essence, as it can only measure nature because nature has already been revealed to us technologically.

> "the essence of technology is not something we make; it is a mode of being, or of revealing."
*(sources: src_008)*

## Primacy of Everyday Understanding

**Canonical statement:** Ordinary, pre-reflective engagement with the world precedes and grounds abstract theoretical knowledge.

**Rationale:** We primarily relate to the world through practical, unthinking engagement with tools and our environment. Abstract ways of knowing, such as logic or theory, are derivative modes that abstract away from our primary practical concern.

> "This meaning would then elucidate ordinary \"prescientific\" understanding, which precedes abstract ways of knowing, such as logic or theory."
*(sources: src_017, src_041)*

## Time and Mortality as the Horizon of Meaning

**Canonical statement:** The concept of time is only relevant to beings that exist in the world, and time finds its ultimate meaning in human mortality.

**Rationale:** Humans are temporally stretched between birth and death. An authentic life requires consciously facing the fear of death, which allows us to consciously design our own lives and realize our true possibilities.

> "Heidegger believes that time finds its meaning in death"
*(sources: src_041, src_021)*

## Authenticity Through Care and Attunement

**Canonical statement:** Human perfection and authentic selfhood are disclosed through fundamental attunements (like anxiety) and accomplished through 'care'.

**Rationale:** Care is the mechanism by which a person becomes free for their ownmost possibilities. Attunements are not mere emotions, but structural ways in which the authentic self and its understanding of being are disclosed.

> "man's perfection -his transformation into that which he can be in Being-free for his own most possibilities (projection) -is 'accomplished' by 'care'"
*(sources: src_022, src_039)*

## The Withdrawal of Being

**Canonical statement:** Being inherently withdraws even as it reveals itself in history.

**Rationale:** Being has multiple historical determinations, but it never gives itself fully. The truth of being cannot be reduced to any single historical concept because of this essential withdrawal.

> "As it discloses itself in beings, being withdraws."
*(sources: src_009)*

## The Social Character of Meaning

**Canonical statement:** The primary disclosure of being is social in character, prescribed by a collective entity rather than isolated individual minds.

**Rationale:** The way we encounter entities and interpret the world is prescribed by the 'anyone-self' (Das Man), which articulates the referential context of significance.

> "the anyone-self... articulates the referential context of significance"
*(sources: src_001)*
