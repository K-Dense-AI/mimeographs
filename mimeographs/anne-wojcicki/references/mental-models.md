# Mental Models of Anne Wojcicki

These mental models illustrate how Anne Wojcicki views the world, particularly regarding healthcare, technology adoption, and systemic disruption.

## Healthcare as a Sick-Care Economy

**Viewing the traditional medical system as a financial engine optimized for illness and margin expansion rather than wellness.**

This model explains why the system resists preventative care: it makes money on diabetes and readmissions, but has no financial incentive to keep someone 'healthy at a hundred.'

> "There’s a lot of reasons why you can make money on diabetes. You can make money on readmissions... Tell me who makes money, if I’m healthy at a hundred, and I will go in that path."

*(sources: src_027, src_010, src_020, src_040, src_043)*

## The Genome as Digital Code

**Treating human DNA as a decipherable digital dataset that represents the individual.**

Rather than viewing DNA as a mystical biological essence, treat it as a digital code (A, C, G, T) that can be aggregated, queried, and used to find patterns for rational drug design.

> "Your genetic is just the digital manifestation of you. And you are really interesting."

*(sources: src_010, src_041, src_047)*

## The Outside Rebellion

**The belief that deeply entrenched, misaligned systems cannot be fixed from within.**

Because the healthcare system's incentives are perfectly designed to monetize illness, true disruption requires building a radical alternative from the outside to force change.

> "You can't change the system from within, so we got to do something more radical from outside."

*(sources: src_001, src_034, src_015)*

## The Crucible Catalyst

**Treating existential threats as forcing functions that compel a company to build stronger foundations.**

When faced with severe challenges (like an FDA shutdown), use the crisis to pivot, build rigorous internal infrastructure, and explore new paths rather than folding.

> "I look at crucible moments as opportunities to pivot and explore a new path. Like, it forces you to look at the world in a different way."

*(sources: src_020)*

## The TIVO Technology Shift

**Evaluating consumer adoption by distinguishing between a shift in delivery versus a shift in concept.**

If people understand the concept (like TV), adoption is easy. If the concept is entirely new (like utilizing personal genetic data), the educational hurdle is massive and must be accounted for.

> "Of course you’re going to want your genetic information. We all want our genetic information. Why would you not want genetic information?"

*(sources: src_036)*

## Historical Absurdity (50-100 Year Rule)

**Evaluating current societal or medical norms by imagining how ridiculous they will look to people in the future.**

Used to contextualize medical paternalism, comparing modern resistance to consumer genetics to past resistance against at-home pregnancy tests or doctors hiding cancer diagnoses from patients.

*(sources: src_034)*

## The "Half Don't Get It" Lens

**Reframing frightening genetic predispositions by focusing on the percentage of people with the mutation who do not develop the disease.**

This shifts the mindset from fatalism to actionable inquiry about protective behaviors and environmental factors.

> "The way to think about it is, half of the people don’t get it. So if half the people aren’t getting it, why? What are they doing with their behavior?"

*(sources: src_044)*
