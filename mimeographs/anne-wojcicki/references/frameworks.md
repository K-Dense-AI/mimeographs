# Frameworks of Anne Wojcicki

These frameworks represent Anne Wojcicki's structured approaches to solving complex problems, from dealing with regulators to launching novel consumer products.

## Navigating Regulated Environments

**A structured approach to transitioning a disruptive startup into a heavily regulated industry.**

1. Recognize regulators as public servants who care about safety.
2. Accept established rules requiring obedience rather than constant questioning.
3. Communicate novel methods using rigorous data, not promises.
4. Genuinely take input from regulators with domain knowledge.

> "The onus is on us a little bit to explain how we're doing it, how that works, and the way they speak... they speak in data. You can't just say, 'Oh no, no no. Trust me, I'm the good guy.'"

*(sources: src_001, src_038, src_034)*

## Research 2.0 (The Consumer-Research Flywheel)

**A self-reinforcing cycle for discovering novel genotype-phenotype correlations at scale.**

1. Provide individuals direct access to their genetic information.
2. Enable users to fill out self-reported phenotypic surveys.
3. Aggregate data into a massive, searchable database.
4. Analyze combined data to find novel associations and return value to the consumer.

> "what we're trying to do is you get access to your genetic information and then you have the ability to fill out a survey... now we're going to start trying to understand what does this genetic information mean"

*(sources: src_015, src_027)*

## Weird to Wonderful

**A strategy for introducing highly novel, complex, or intimidating products to the consumer market.**

1. Acknowledge the core product might seem 'weird' to average consumers.
2. Identify a 'wonderful', highly relatable use case (e.g., ancestry).
3. Use this positive use case to educate, build engagement, and spark sharing before introducing heavier features (e.g., disease risk).

> "One of the things my marketing team will say is, 'You need to take it from the weird to the wonderful.'"

*(sources: src_041)*

## Navigating the Trough of Sorrow

**A leadership framework for managing a team when initial launch momentum fades.**

1. Acknowledge the slow period without letting the team wallow in today's metrics.
2. Shift focus to the long-term vision (e.g., the 2-year state).
3. Outline specific steps to reach that state.
4. Gather market feedback to adjust messaging and educate consumers.

> "one of the most important things is not to wallow on today but to wallow on like okay in two years this is what its gonna look like and these are the steps I need to do to get there"

*(sources: src_001, src_034)*
