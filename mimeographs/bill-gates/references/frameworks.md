Structured methodologies Bill Gates uses to evaluate investments, allocate resources, and measure progress.

## The Green Premium Assessment

**Canonical statement:** A framework for evaluating climate solutions by calculating the extra cost of a zero-carbon alternative compared to its fossil-fuel counterpart.

**Rationale:** To solve climate change, the economic barrier to adopting clean technology must be eliminated. This framework dictates whether capital should be spent on deployment (if the premium is low) or on R&D (if the premium is high).

**Steps:**
1. Compute the 'green premium' across all emission categories.
2. If the premium is low, adopt the technology and use policy to scale it.
3. If the premium is high, direct R&D and patient capital to innovate and drive the cost down to zero.

> "I would say the key metric to say we’re going to get to zero is how much we’re bringing these premiums down to an acceptable level."

## Data-Driven Resource Allocation

**Canonical statement:** A method for budgeting finite philanthropic or public health resources to maximize lives saved.

**Rationale:** Without specific data on disease burdens and pathogens, resources are wasted. Granular measurement allows organizations to track progress and target interventions ecologically.

**Steps:**
1. Identify disease burden using metrics like DALYs.
2. Break down broad symptoms into specific pathogens.
3. Evaluate available interventions for cost-effectiveness.
4. Direct funding to specific causes where dollars save the most lives.

> "The foundation literally takes the death burden, the disability burden, and uses that to prioritize our work."

*(sources: src_010)*

## 100-Day Outbreak Containment Strategy

**Canonical statement:** A rapid-response blueprint to prevent localized infectious diseases from becoming global pandemics.

**Rationale:** Outbreaks are inevitable acts of nature, but pandemics are optional failures of human preparation. Speed is the ultimate weapon in containment.

**Steps:**
1. Rapidly identify the threat and share data globally.
2. Deploy blunt tools on hand (quarantines, distancing).
3. Innovators immediately develop new tests and vaccines using pre-agreed trials.
4. Rapidly manufacture and deliver globally within 100 days.

> "The goal is to contain outbreaks within the first 100 days before they ever have the chance to spread around the world."

*(sources: src_050)*

## Theory of Change for Institution Building

**Canonical statement:** A foundational approach to building highly impactful organizations, whether in technology or philanthropy.

**Rationale:** Success requires maniacal focus on a core innovation and the resilience to endure inevitable setbacks over a long time horizon.

**Steps:**
1. Identify what the core innovation is going to be.
2. Stick to it maniacally.
3. Build a dedicated team behind that innovation.
4. Endure inevitable setbacks to eventually reach success.

> "Find what an innovation is going to be really stick to it build a team behind that uh have some setbacks and success is a you know kind of a theory of change."

*(sources: src_017)*

## The AP Biology Test for AI

**Canonical statement:** A benchmark for evaluating whether an AI model has achieved complex logic and broad understanding.

**Rationale:** True intelligence requires reasoning through complex, unseen problems rather than simply regurgitating memorized training data.

**Steps:**
1. Select an advanced placement biology exam question not in the model's training set.
2. Ask the AI to provide a fully reasoned answer.
3. Evaluate if it demonstrates complex logic beyond simply regurgitating a textbook.

> "If you can pass an advanced placement biology exam where you take a question that’s not part of the training set... and give fully reasoned answers... then you will really get my attention."

*(sources: src_039)*
