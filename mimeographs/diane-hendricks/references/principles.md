This document outlines the core principles that drive Diane Hendricks's approach to business, resilience, and community building.

## The American Dream is Achievable
The American Dream is accessible to anyone, regardless of their background, through relentless hard work and determination.

Hendricks views her own trajectory from a teenage single mother without a college degree to a billionaire business owner as living proof that the US system works for those who apply themselves and refuse to see closed doors.

> "I'm living proof the American dream is possible through hard work and determination."
(sources: src_010, src_001, src_009, src_024)

## Treat Blue-Collar Workers as Respected Entrepreneurs
Contractors and tradespeople deserve the same professional treatment, choices, and respect as any other business owner.

This customer-centric empathy was the foundational insight for ABC Supply. By recognizing that a contractor in a pickup truck is running a legitimate business, they built a wholesale model that catered specifically to their dignity and operational needs.

> "Even though they might be in a pickup truck, they were running a business and we appreciated the hard work it took to be a roofing contractor."
(sources: src_026, src_003, src_012)

## Fight Rather Than Fold
When faced with devastating personal and professional loss, you must consciously choose to fight and persevere rather than give up.

After the sudden death of her husband and co-founder, she faced a critical juncture. Instead of selling the company or stepping back, she chose to take on the burden, ultimately quadrupling the size of her business.

> "I had to decide whether to fight or fold. And I chose to fight."
(sources: src_010, src_009, src_011)

## Solve Your Own Customer Pain Points
Build businesses that solve the exact supply chain gaps and problems you faced as a customer in that industry.

As contractors, Hendricks and her husband struggled to source specific products nationally because manufacturers only distributed locally. They built ABC Supply to provide the exact nationwide distribution network they had needed themselves.

> "we did everything that we could to make the contractor have what we hadn't been provided for as contractors"
(sources: src_011, src_012)

## Community Investment Elevates Everyone
Adding value to a community through local infrastructure and business investment creates a ripple effect that encourages others to improve and thrive.

She believes in remaining deeply committed to the city where a business is founded. By investing in abandoned buildings and core infrastructure, secondary businesses like hotels and restaurants are attracted, revitalizing the entire ecosystem.

> "when you start adding value to a community everybody picks up their game"
(sources: src_011, src_019, src_026, src_005)

## Financial Responsibility is Paramount
Entrepreneurs must prioritize financial responsibility, ensuring partners and employees are paid before the founder.

Taking on financial partners requires a strict commitment to paying them back to protect your reputation. The founder is at the bottom of the payment hierarchy.

> "I personally take financial responsibility as the number one priority."
(sources: src_000)
