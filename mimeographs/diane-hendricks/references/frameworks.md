This document details the step-by-step frameworks Diane Hendricks uses to evaluate acquisitions and build community workforce programs.

## Acquisition Due Diligence
A strict evaluation process for potential acquisitions to ensure financial viability and strategic necessity.

Steps include:
1. **Look under the covers twice:** Perform exhaustive due diligence to see exactly what liabilities, assets, and cultural elements you are buying.
2. **Verify financial sense:** Ensure the numbers work strictly on their own merits without relying on overly optimistic future projections.
3. **Ensure genuine need:** Confirm there is a real, tangible need for this acquisition in the market, not just a desire for corporate scale.

> "When you do Acquisitions you learn the word diligence because you've got to look under the covers more than once and see what you're buying to make sure that it makes Financial sense and is there really a need"
(sources: src_011)

## Hands-On Vocational Training Model
A framework for partnering with local schools to bridge the skills gap by combining academic learning with real-world project execution.

Steps include:
1. **Teach the academics:** Start with classroom instruction on the fundamentals of the trade.
2. **Provide hands-on application:** Have students execute a real-world project (e.g., demolishing and rebuilding a home) guided by professionals.
3. **Delegate budget management:** Put students in charge of managing the project's budget to teach financial responsibility and project management.
4. **Recycle the proceeds:** Sell the completed project and return the proceeds to the school to fund the program for the next cohort.
(sources: src_003)
