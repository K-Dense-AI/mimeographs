This document catalogs the mental models and heuristics Diane Hendricks uses to navigate markets, crises, and community development.

## Fight or Fold
A binary decision-making lens used during moments of extreme crisis or loss, forcing a clear choice between giving up entirely or taking on the burden to continue forward. Applied when her husband and co-founder died, leaving her to decide the fate of her family and her massive company. It cuts through the complexity of grief and business turmoil.

> "I had to decide whether to fight or fold. And I chose to fight."
(sources: src_010, src_009)

## The Pickup Truck Business Owner
Viewing blue-collar workers not merely as laborers, but as legitimate entrepreneurs who require professional-grade services, choices, and respect. This mental model shifted the paradigm in roofing distribution, treating contractors with the dignity of corporate executives.

> "Even though they might be in a pickup truck, they were running a business and we appreciated the hard work it took to be a roofing contractor."
(sources: src_003)

## Founder as Customer
Looking at an industry from the perspective of the end-user to identify what the current supply chain fails to provide. Realizing that manufacturers acting as their own distributors created regional pockets, preventing contractors from getting specified products for national jobs.

> "we did everything that we could to make the contractor have what we hadn't been provided for as contractors"
(sources: src_012)

## Counter-Cyclical Expansion
Viewing industry turmoil and market collapses as prime opportunities to acquire rivals at fire-sale prices. She orchestrated ABC Supply's biggest acquisition by buying rival Bradco in 2010 during the real estate market collapse.
(sources: src_024)

## Community as an Ecosystem
Viewing a city's revitalization as an interconnected system where investing in core infrastructure breathes life back into the area, attracting secondary businesses. Buying bankrupt industrial buildings and turning them into thriving centers brings life back to the city and elevates the entire local economy.

> "when you start adding value to a community everybody picks up their game"
(sources: src_011)

## The Entrepreneur's Payment Hierarchy
A lens for viewing business cash flow where the founder is at the very bottom of the priority list for getting paid. Everyone and everything else—employees, vendors, financial partners—must be paid before the entrepreneur takes a cut.

> "Take responsibility for your actions and keep in mind everyone and everything gets paid before you."
(sources: src_000)
