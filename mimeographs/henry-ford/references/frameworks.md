This document details the operational and economic frameworks Henry Ford used to scale production and manage crises.

## Mass-Market Economics
A strategy for transforming a luxury good into a basic necessity for the general public.
Steps:
1. Set the product price low to capture the widest possible market.
2. Achieve the necessary cost reductions through extreme volume and production efficiency.
3. Pay workers a living wage so they have the purchasing power to buy the product.
> "...stressed low pricing... in order to capture the widest possible market and then met the price by volume and efficiency."
*(sources: src_003, src_006)*

## Moving Assembly Line Production
A manufacturing process designed to drastically cut production time when demand outpaces traditional capacity.
Steps:
1. Break down the manufacturing process into single, specific tasks.
2. Assign each worker to one fixed location.
3. Move the product past the stationary workers to minimize wasted time and motion.
*(sources: src_000, src_028)*

## Just-In-Time Production System
A method to increase cash flow and reduce inventory costs during financial pressure.
Steps:
1. Pressure suppliers to ensure on-time delivery of essential components.
2. Reduce inventory levels significantly to free up capital.
3. Pressure dealers to accelerate sales.
4. Use the freed-up capital to pay off outstanding loans.
*(sources: src_047)*
