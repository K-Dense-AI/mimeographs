This document catalogs the mental models Judith Butler employs to analyze social dynamics, rhetoric, and collective action.

## Gender Performativity
Viewing gender not as an innate fact or fake performance, but as a "performative" act that brings reality into being, similar to a judge declaring a couple married. Unlike theatrical acting where the act is de-realized, gender performance in real life lacks these boundaries and carries real punitive risks for deviating from the script.
> "when we talk about performative, we're talking about an act that makes something come into being or an act that has real consequences." (sources: src_038, src_000, src_010)

## Inadvertent Confession (Authoritarian Projection)
A mechanism where an attacking group accuses their target of the exact oppressive actions they themselves are committing. For example, authoritarian governments claim "gender ideology" will strip people of their rights, while they actively strip trans people and women of their legal rights and bodily autonomy.
> "Most of these allegations that are made by the Trump Administration are also confessions of what they themselves are doing in the very act." (sources: src_015, src_020)

## Furious Nostalgia / Fantasy of Restoration
Understanding right-wing populist appeal as a deeply emotional, aggressive desire to restore a lost social hierarchy and dominance, rather than mere economic anxiety. It explains why alienated workers are drawn to promises of returning to a time when "men were men" and white men were unburdened by equality measures.
> "The resentment thing is huge as is what I would call a furious Nostalgia just furious Nostalgia like I really want that back I really want my place back." (sources: src_020, src_011)

## Ecstasy (Being Beside Oneself)
A lens for viewing community and collectivity not through static identity, but through shared experiences of being overwhelmed or displaced by extreme emotion (grief, rage, passion) or shared crisis. It illustrates the fundamental dependency individuals have on others, binding people together into a community of strangers responding to a shared predicament.
> "In a sense, a predicament is to understand what kind of community is composed of those who are beside themselves." (sources: src_006, src_030)

## Strategic Abandonment
The state's withdrawal from providing social welfare, forcing populations into precarity while shifting the burden of survival entirely onto the traditional family and churches. This creates the conditions where the traditional family is propped up as the only safety net, making any perceived threat to the family (like "gender") feel like a threat to survival itself.
> "as neoliberal economic policies devastate the work lives and the sense of Futurity for many people... the turn against gender is a way of shoring up a traditional sense of place and privilege" (sources: src_012)
