# Immanuel Kant's Frameworks

Kant developed structured, step-by-step methods for evaluating moral duties, political structures, and the limits of human cognition.

## The Categorical Imperative Test

A decision framework for determining whether a proposed action or personal rule is morally permissible.

**Steps:**
1. Identify your maxim (the subjective principle behind your action).
2. Imagine if that maxim were a universal law that everyone followed.
3. Determine if universalizing it would create a logical contradiction that undoes the action's premise.
4. If it cannot be willed as a universal law, the action is morally wrong.

> "Act only in accordance with that maxim through which you can at the same time will that it become a universal law."
*(sources: src_016, src_040)*

## Public vs. Private Use of Reason

A framework for balancing civic obedience with intellectual freedom.

**Steps:**
1. Identify the context: are you acting in a civic post (private use) or as a scholar addressing the public (public use)?
2. If in a civic post, remain passive and obey orders to ensure institutional function.
3. If acting as a scholar, exercise unlimited freedom to publish criticisms and propose improvements.

> "By \"public use of one's reason\" I mean that use which a man, as scholar , makes of it before the reading public. I call \"private use\" that use which a man makes of his reason in a civic post"
*(sources: src_002)*

## Critique of Reason / Transcendental Deduction

A method for determining the valid scope of human knowledge by examining the limits of our cognitive powers.

**Steps:**
1. Acknowledge that all experience must be ascribed to a single identical subject.
2. Identify the a priori forms of intuition (space and time) and categories of understanding.
3. Restrict the application of these a priori concepts strictly to the phenomenal world (appearances), denying their application to things in themselves.

> "'Critique,' in Kant's sense of the term, consists in examining the scope and limits of our cognitive powers... in order to decide to what extent, if any, metaphysics is possible for us human beings."
*(sources: src_004, src_023)*

## The Perpetual Peace Program

A structured political program for governments to transition from international hostility to permanent peace.

**Steps:**
1. Implement Preliminary Articles (abolish secret treaties, standing armies, and national debts for war).
2. Establish Definitive Articles: ensure all states have republican constitutions.
3. Form a federation of these free states.
4. Guarantee universal hospitality for global citizens.

> "Kant proposed a peace program to be implemented by governments. The \"Preliminary Articles\" described these steps that should be taken immediately... Three Definitive Articles would provide not merely a cessation of hostilities but a foundation on which to build a peace."
*(sources: src_056)*
