# Mental Models

These mental models form the lens through which Yoshua Bengio views artificial intelligence, its capabilities, and its risks.

## Distributed Representations
Mapping discrete symbols into a continuous vector space where geometric proximity represents semantic similarity, allowing models to generalize to unseen configurations. Instead of breaking the input space into mutually exclusive regions, distributed representations use intersecting half-planes. This allows the number of distinguishable regions to grow exponentially with the number of parameters.
> "We propose to fight the curse of dimensionality by learning a distributed representation for words which allows each training sentence to inform the model about an exponential number of semantically neighboring sentences."
*(sources: src_048, src_002, src_017, src_025)*

## The Baby Tiger Metaphor
Training deep learning AI is not like programming explicit rules; it is akin to raising a wild animal. You can curate its experiences, but you cannot perfectly predict its adult behavior. A cute, fun baby tiger might grow up to be a friendly companion, or it might become a dangerous predator. This illustrates the 'black box' problem of modern AI.
> "When you have a cute baby tiger and it's nice and fun, you don't know if it's going to become a dangerous adult tiger or a good friendly one."
*(sources: src_024, src_029, src_033, src_021)*

## Agentic vs. Non-Agentic AI
A lens for evaluating AI safety based on whether a system autonomously pursues goals or merely observes and explains. Agentic AI (systems that autonomously plan, act, and pursue goals) is inherently risky and prone to misalignment and reward hacking. Non-agentic AI (systems that observe, explain, and answer questions) is safer and can capture the benefits of AI without catastrophic risks.
*(sources: src_010, src_051)*

## Jagged Intelligence
AI intelligence is not one-dimensional like human IQ; it can be vastly superhuman in specific skills while remaining severely deficient in others. An AI can master 200 languages and pass PhD-level exams across all disciplines, yet remain incapable of planning more than an hour ahead, much like a six-year-old.
*(sources: src_029, src_033, src_021)*

## Disentangling Explanatory Factors
Viewing raw data as a tangled mixture of simple, high-level abstract concepts. The goal of representation learning is to disentangle these underlying causal variables. The real world is generated by a set of somewhat independent factors of variation. Good representation learning seeks to separate these factors so they can be reused.
> "The job of deep learning is really to disentangle that mess in order to recover those high-level concepts."
*(sources: src_045, src_002, src_017)*

## The Nuclear Technology Analogy
Managing the global proliferation of dangerous AI is similar to the Cold War nuclear arms race. Rival superpowers must realize that mutual survival requires international treaties, strict guardrails, and verification mechanisms to ensure safety, just as the global community handled nuclear power and weapons.
> "Let’s make sure that we discuss these questions internationally just like we’ve done for nuclear power and nuclear weapons."
*(sources: src_029, src_052)*
