# Heuristics and Rules of Thumb

Yoshua Bengio applies these practical rules of thumb to navigate deep learning research and AI safety.

## Explain, Don't Act
To build safe AI, focus on building systems that explain the world and quantify uncertainty, rather than systems that take actions to imitate or please humans.
> "This system is designed to explain the world from observations, as opposed to taking actions in it to imitate or please humans."
*(sources: src_010, src_051)*

## The 0.1% Extinction Rule
If an experiment or technological development has even a 0.1% chance of destroying humanity, do not do it.
> "if you’re doing something, say a scientific experiment, and it could turn out really, really bad, like people could die, some catastrophe could happen, then you should not do it."
*(sources: src_033, src_021)*

## Fight Exponentials with Exponentials
When attacked by the exponential curse of dimensionality, use the exponential gains of compositionality, distributed representations, and depth to fight it.
> "When you're attacked by an exponential just use more Exponential's to fight it right"
*(sources: src_017)*

## Intuition Precedes Math
Don't be afraid of the math; develop the intuitive understanding of the system first, and the mathematical formalization will become much easier to grasp. Formalize logically first so you can convince someone it makes sense, then use math to make it tighter.
> "don't be afraid by the math just you know just develop the intuitions and then the math become really easier to understand"
*(sources: src_025)*

## The Zero Training Error Check
If you cannot quickly reach zero training error on a small subset of your data, you likely have a bug or a bad learning rate.
> "train on the small data set and verify you can reach your training error quickly"
*(sources: src_002)*

## Assume Sycophancy
Assume AI chatbots are lying to please you when you ask for feedback on your own ideas.
*(sources: src_021)*

## Pause and Govern
When facing unprecedented technological risks: raise the flag, pause, think, and work on governance.
> "We have to raise the flag, we need to pause, we need to think, we need to work around solutions and governance."
*(sources: src_052)*
