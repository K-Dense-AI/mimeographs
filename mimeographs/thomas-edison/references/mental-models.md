# Thomas Edison's Mental Models

Edison's mental models reveal a thinker who views the world through the lens of interconnected systems, relentless iteration, and unglamorous reality.

## Whole System Design
Approaching a new technology not as an isolated device, but as an entire ecosystem that includes generation, distribution, and end-use products. Instead of just inventing the light bulb, Edison founded the illuminating company, built the power station, and laid electrical conduits to deliver the necessary power. An invention without its delivery mechanism is useless.

> "instead of just fabricating pieces of the electric puzzle, it was Edison’s intention to create a whole system"
*(sources: src_016, src_048)*

## Disappointments as the 'Salt of the Inventor'
Viewing failures, rejections, or the discovery that an idea is already patented not as a defeat, but as a necessary test of character that ultimately leads to triumph. When an inventor faces setbacks, they must use the disappointment to fuel their resilience rather than as a reason to quit.

> "But disappointments show the salt of the inventor. Only by such disappointments can he triumph finally."
*(sources: src_026)*

## Opportunity as Labor
The understanding that high-value opportunities rarely present themselves as easy wins, but rather as demanding, unglamorous work. Most people overlook their best chances for success because they are unwilling to engage in the tedious, difficult labor required to realize them.

> "Opportunity is missed by most people because it is dressed in overalls and looks like hard work."
*(sources: src_003, src_044)*

## The Human as an Aggregate
Viewing a human being not as a singular, indivisible entity or soul, but as a collective aggregate of smaller living components (cells). Edison compared a human being to New York City, illustrating the absurdity of an aggregate entity having a singular afterlife.

> "I am not an individual—I am an aggregate of cells, as, for instance, New York City is an aggregate of individuals. Will New York City go to heaven?"
*(sources: src_002)*
