# Thomas Edison's Frameworks

Edison operationalized invention. He moved it out of the garage and into the factory, creating repeatable frameworks for generating commercial breakthroughs.

## The Industrial Invention Factory
A systematic approach to producing marketable products more productively than a lone inventor could achieve. Edison realized that combining specialized teams with a clear commercial directive was vastly superior to isolated tinkering.

**Steps:**
1. Start with a definite idea of a practical result.
2. Collect all possible scientific and commercial data.
3. Assign dedicated teams of researchers to work on specific projects.
4. Systematically test materials and iterate until a viable product is found.
5. Develop the surrounding systems required to commercialize the product.

> "I always start out with a definite idea of accomplishing a certain result. I collect all the data possible... I then proceed to sketch out every possible and probable way of attaining results and carry it to success by experimenting."

## Analogous System Design
Using a prevailing, widely adopted system as a structural and economic benchmark to guide the development of a new, disruptive technology. By designing a new system to mimic the familiar parameters of an existing one, you ensure the new technology will be familiar to users and commercially competitive.

**How to apply:**
When attempting to replace an incumbent technology (like gas lighting), map out its exact economic and structural parameters. Design the new technology (electric lighting) to fit into those exact same usage patterns (e.g., individual subdivision of lights) so the consumer behavior doesn't have to change, only the underlying utility.
