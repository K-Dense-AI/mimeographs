This document catalogs the mental models Mary Wollstonecraft uses to perceive power, society, and human development.

## Neo-Roman Liberty (Master/Slave Dynamic)
A lens for understanding freedom not merely as non-interference, but as independence from arbitrary power. If you live subject to another's will, you are a slave, regardless of how kindly you are treated.
> "But if nonetheless, they do that at the mercy of their master, of another, if they’re subject to the arbitrary power of another, then they are unfree."
*(sources: src_031)*

## The Barren Blooming (Flowers in Over-Rich Soil)
A metaphor for how society currently educates women. By focusing entirely on superficial beauty and allure rather than intellectual strength, society produces a creature that is temporarily pleasing but ultimately weak and useless. Strength and usefulness are sacrificed to beauty.
*(sources: src_043)*

## Subjectivity Construction
The lens of viewing personal identity, desires, and sense of self not as innate, but as intimately shaped and constructed by surrounding social and patriarchal structures. Women in patriarchal societies are conditioned to desire the conditions of their own subordination.
*(sources: src_045)*

## The Personal is Political
The understanding that private, domestic struggles and psychological breakdowns are not individual failings but direct functions of structural inequality and power relations.
*(sources: src_031)*

## The Chain of Despots
A lens for understanding hierarchical organizations (like the military or clergy) where individuals do not use their own reason, but merely obey those above them and command those below them. This creates a structure devoid of individual moral character.
*(sources: src_043)*
