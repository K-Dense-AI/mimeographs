This document outlines the core principles that drive Mary Wollstonecraft's reasoning, focusing on education, independence, reason, and equality.

## Rational Education as the Foundation of Equality
Women must be granted equal, rational education to become capable citizens, workers, and companions. Without this, they are reduced to mere ornaments and halt the progress of societal knowledge and virtue. Education is a political tool; cultivating moral character and rational judgment is required for a prosperous state.
> "Contending for the rights of woman, my main argument is built on this simple principle, that if she be not prepared by education to become the companion of man, she will stop the progress of knowledge and virtue; for truth must be common to all"
*(sources: src_001, src_000, src_013, src_009, src_027, src_049, src_043, src_044, src_033)*

## True Freedom as Independence from Arbitrary Power
Freedom is not merely non-interference, but absolute independence from the arbitrary will of another. For women, this necessitates financial independence and the ability to earn their own subsistence. Even if a master or husband is kind, remaining dependent on his will makes one unfree.
> "I only desire to subsist, without being dependant on the caprice of an fellow creature"
*(sources: src_031, src_024, src_033)*

## The Primacy of Reason for Virtue
Human pre-eminence consists in reason, and true virtue requires rational judgment rather than being blown about by excessive sensibility or emotion. While reason and feeling should inform each other, allowing feelings to completely override reason prevents rational thought.
> "What does man’s pre-eminence over the lower animals consist in? The answer is as clear as ‘A half is less than the whole’; it consists in reason. What acquirement raises one being above another? We spontaneously reply: virtue."
*(sources: src_038, src_008, src_039, src_043)*

## The Social Construction of Gender and Virtue
Differences in capability or virtue between the sexes are not innate biological traits, but the result of corrupt societal conditioning, unequal education, and patriarchal structures. There are no "sexual virtues"; moral duties are shared equally by all human beings.
> "Make them free and they will quickly become wise and virtuous."
*(sources: src_031, src_033, src_039, src_045, src_030)*
