# Core Principles

This document outlines the foundational beliefs and decision rules that drive Andrew Ng's approach to AI, education, and business.

## Govern AI applications, not AI technology
Safety is a function of the application, not the underlying technology. Regulating base foundation models stifles open-source innovation and misses the actual risks, which depend entirely on how the AI is implemented downstream. Like an electric motor, AI is a general-purpose tool. It cannot be inherently safe or unsafe; it depends entirely on whether it is used responsibly in a specific application.
> "we should be focus on governing the applications because risks depend much more on the application not on the technology."
*(sources: src_013, src_021, src_052, src_016)*

## Everyone should learn to code in the AI era
As AI makes coding easier and more accessible, the ability to steer a computer to do exactly what you want becomes a superpower for all roles, not just software engineers. Advising people not to learn to code because AI will automate it is terrible career advice. People who use AI to code will be vastly more productive.
> "Some senior business leaders were recently advising others to not learn to code on the grounds that AI will automate coding. We’ll look back on that as some of the worst career advice ever given."
*(sources: src_021, src_014, src_016)*

## AI automates tasks, not jobs
Jobs are composed of many distinct tasks. AI is best understood and implemented by analyzing work at the task level to see where it can automate or augment, rather than viewing it as a wholesale replacement for human jobs. Automating 20-30% of a worker's tasks makes them more productive and increases the marginal value of their labor.
> "from a business perspective I find it more useful to think of AI as timating tasks rather than jobs because jobs are made out of many tasks"
*(sources: src_013, src_052)*

## The application layer must generate the most value
For the underlying AI infrastructure (semiconductors, cloud, foundation models) to succeed economically, the applications built on top must generate enough revenue to pay for those lower layers. Identifying and building these specific, valuable applications is the most important work ahead.
> "we need the applications to generate even even more Revenue so that they can afford to pay the technology layers underneath them"
*(sources: src_013, src_052)*

## Drive the cost of proof-of-concepts to zero
Because AI accelerates prototyping by 10x or more, teams should focus on building many cheap prototypes to find the few great ideas, rather than trying to force every prototype into production. Execution speed is the strongest predictor of a startup's success.
> "I would rather focus on driving the cost of those 18 proof of concepts towards zero. And this is becoming a new mechanism for teams to invent."
*(sources: src_014, src_021)*

## Technology that unlocks creativity creates jobs
Historically, whenever technology unlocks human creativity, it expands opportunities and creates far more jobs than it destroys. The world is generally better off with more intelligence in it.
> "Whenever technology unlocks human creativity, it creates far more jobs than it destroys."
*(sources: src_009, src_052)*

## In times of uncertainty, build on stable foundations
During periods of economic and technological flux, personal skills and professional communities are the resilient assets most likely to weather shifts in business and society.
> "In times of economic and technological uncertainty, build on things that tend to remain stable. Community and skills are likely to weather shifts in business and society."
*(sources: src_002, src_009)*

## AI-native software development requires generalists
As AI accelerates the coding process, software engineering teams operate differently, requiring teammates to take on a wider variety of roles.
> "As AI accelerates software development, teammates must play a wider variey of roles AI-native software engineering teams operate very differently than traditional teams."
*(sources: src_002, src_009)*

## Fast feedback loops are critical for iteration
Waiting months for traditional feedback stalls progress. Agentic AI workflows can provide rapid, actionable feedback to tighten iteration cycles.
> "Their feedback loop -- waiting ~6 months for feedback each time -- was painfully slow. We wanted to see if an agentic workflow can help researchers iterate faster."
*(sources: src_049, src_014)*

## Scaling data and compute drives performance
Scaling up data and compute infrastructure leads to massive, predictable performance improvements in AI capabilities.
> "Using Google's compute infrastructure, we started by proving that scaling up data and compute leads to huge performance improvements"
*(sources: src_008)*

## Apply a data-centric approach to ML
Model performance can be significantly improved by focusing on tuning the data itself through synthesis or augmentation, rather than solely tuning the model architecture.
> "Apply “data-centric AI” to not only tune your model but tune your data (using data synthesis or data augmentation) to improve your model’s performance."
*(sources: src_028)*
