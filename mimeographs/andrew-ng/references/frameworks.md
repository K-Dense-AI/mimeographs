# Frameworks

These are the step-by-step processes Andrew Ng uses to evaluate opportunities, build products, and implement AI.

## Agentic Workflow Iteration
A loop for generating high-quality, complex output from an LLM by mimicking human cognitive processes of research, drafting, and revision.

**Steps:**
1. Prompt the AI to write an outline.
2. Have the AI perform web research to fetch context.
3. Generate a first draft.
4. Have the AI read, critique, and revise the draft.
5. Repeat the loop iteratively to improve the work product.

> "we end up with this iterative workflow where your model does some thinking and some research does some revision goes back to do more thinking and by going around this loop many times."
*(sources: src_021, src_013)*

## Task-Based Automation Analysis
A method for evaluating how AI will impact a specific job, business, or industry by breaking roles down into component tasks.

**Steps:**
1. Look at what people are doing in a specific sector.
2. Break the jobs down into their component tasks.
3. Identify the subset of tasks that are amenable to AI automation.
4. Automate those specific tasks to free up workers to focus on the rest of their job and increase productivity.

> "break jobs down into tasks, then usually there can often be a subset of tasks. There's some that are amenable to AI automation and that recipe is helping a lot of businesses create value"
*(sources: src_052, src_013)*

## Rapid Product Feedback Hierarchy
A tiered approach to quickly validating product decisions, moving from the fastest (but potentially less accurate) to the slowest methods.

**Steps:**
1. Rely on your own gut instinct as a subject matter expert.
2. Ask 3 friends or teammates to play with the product.
3. Sit in a high-traffic area and respectfully ask 3-10 strangers for feedback.
4. Send prototypes to 100 testers.
5. Run an A/B test (the slowest method).
*(sources: src_021, src_014)*

## Corporate AI Innovation Process
A systematic five-step process for large corporations to identify and implement AI opportunities.

**Steps:**
1. Provide executive training on AI technology.
2. Brainstorm AI application opportunities using a task-based analysis.
3. Conduct diligence on project feasibility and value.
4. Prioritize projects and make build vs. buy vs. spin-out decisions.
5. Invest in long-term AI strategy and workforce training.
*(sources: src_013)*
