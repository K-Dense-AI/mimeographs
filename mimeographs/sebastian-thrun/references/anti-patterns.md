# Anti-Patterns

These are the practices and mindsets Sebastian Thrun explicitly warns against, as they stifle innovation and execution.

## Arguing Component Architectures
Debating hypothetical algorithms and architectures instead of building an end-to-end system. People debate algorithms endlessly. Building an end-to-end system immediately reveals the actual, practical problems you need to solve.
*(sources: src_015)*

## Treating Employees Like Computers
Expecting people to execute commands flawlessly like code. People have emotions, pride, and aspirations. Telling them what to do like a machine causes resentment and failure.
> "many, many engineers turn management believe they can treat their team just the same way with a computer. And it just doesn't work that way."
*(sources: src_029, src_016)*

## Developing Until the Deadline
Working on software development until the last second before a deadline. It leaves trivial but fatal bugs undiscovered. Teams complain they 'just needed one more week' because they didn't freeze the system early enough to test it properly.
*(sources: src_016)*

## Preemptive Regulation
Rashly regulating AI out of premature fear before understanding the actual abuses. It destroys the chances of the technology to make humanity stronger and solve big problems before those possibilities are fully explored.
> "My greatest worry is that society destroys artificial intelligence through rash regulation."
*(sources: src_000, src_006)*

## Academic Publishing in Product Labs
Incentivizing academic publishing in a product-focused innovation lab. It puts researchers in a 'hamster wheel' of optimizing for personal brand and half-year conference cycles, which is inconsistent with the longevity required to carry a project through to a real product.
*(sources: src_020)*

## Focusing on Methods Over Problems
Academics getting trapped solving abstract component problems to impress peers. This causes researchers to miss out on massive societal problems (like saving a million lives in traffic) because they aren't building end-to-end systems.
*(sources: src_029, src_016)*

## Relying on Introspection for AI Design
Designing AI systems based on how humans introspectively believe they think. Introspection is flawed; humans believe they are logical, but decisions are often emotional or subconscious. This misled generations of early AI researchers into focusing solely on logic.
*(sources: src_015)*

## The Hero Leader
Leaders who try to take all the credit and act as the hero of the team. Taking all the credit stifles world-class execution; leaders should instead act as coaches and janitors to empower their team.
*(sources: src_032)*
