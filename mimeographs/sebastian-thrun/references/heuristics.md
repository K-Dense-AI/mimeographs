# Heuristics and Rules of Thumb

These are the pithy rules of thumb Sebastian Thrun applies day-to-day in engineering and leadership.

## Assume Good Intentions
Assume every person has incredibly good intentions and wants to contribute.
> "I've really never really met a person with bad intentions. I believe every person wants to contribute."
*(sources: src_029, src_016)*

## Focus on the Weakest Link
Continuously focus engineering efforts exclusively on identifying and improving the weakest part of the system.
> "As long as you focus on improving the weakest part of the system, you eventually build a really great system."
*(sources: src_029, src_016)*

## Freeze Software Early
Freeze all software development a full month before a major deadline to focus entirely on testing.
*(sources: src_029, src_016)*

## The 1% Rule of Invention
Assume that only 1% of interesting things have been invented yet; 99% of stuff has not been invented yet.
> "I deeply believe that only 1% of interesting things have been invented yet. And 99% of stuff has not been invented yet."
*(sources: src_020, src_032)*

## Aim High to Shoot High
If you don't aim high, you won't shoot high. Audacious goals attract better talent and energy.
> "If you don’t aim high, you won’t shoot high."
*(sources: src_032)*

## Build to Understand
To think like an AI professional, just build a system and try it out.
*(sources: src_015)*

## Define 'Done'
Know exactly what 'done' means to gain an execution advantage. Written, non-negotiable milestones eliminate arguments.
*(sources: src_032)*

## Desperation Hiring
Hire only when you are desperate.
*(sources: src_020)*

## Product Over Papers
Stop publishing papers if you want to build a product.
> "You can have a thousand people reference your paper or a billion people use your product and you can only pick one. Which one is it going to be?"
*(sources: src_020)*
