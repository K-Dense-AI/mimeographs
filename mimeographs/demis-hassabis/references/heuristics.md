# Heuristics and Rules of Thumb

Demis Hassabis relies on these practical heuristics to guide research direction, manage AI scaling, and ensure responsible development.

## Target Root Node Problems
Focus your efforts on foundational problems that, if solved, automatically unlock entire new branches of research and application.
> "If you think of the tree of all knowledge. These are kind of root node problems which if you cracked it, it would unlock a whole branch of new research or new applications." (sources: src_016, src_019)

## Use Games as Proving Grounds
Pick training games that are in the sweet spot: not so easy that they are trivial to solve, but not so hard that you can't detect any progress. Go up the ladder of complexity by going through the different eras of games. (sources: src_012, src_023, src_026)

## Immerse to Become Superpowered
Immerse yourself completely in the latest AI tools to leverage the capability overhang and gain 'superpowers' in your field.
> "I would immerse myself in every tool available and just become almost like superpowered." (sources: src_016, src_014)

## Search In Silico, Validate In Vitro
Perform massive combinatorial searches and optimizations virtually, and only move to the physical wet lab for final validation. (sources: src_016)

## Remove Human Heuristics
To find truly optimal solutions, remove human-crafted knowledge and biases from the system and let it learn from scratch.
> "removing any knowledge human crafted knowledge both in the data and in the uh any of the kind of huristics that we've given the system... starts off random" (sources: src_016)

## Plan for Success Decades in Advance
Anticipate the transformative consequences of your technology decades in advance and bake ethics and safety into the core of the project from the beginning. (sources: src_026, src_031)

## Look to Neuroscience for Principles, Not Prototypes
Study the brain for general principles of intelligence (like reinforcement learning), not to copy its exact literal biological mechanisms. (sources: src_033)

## Scaling is an Artform
Adjust the recipe and hyperparameters at each new scale rather than just repeating them. One order of magnitude is probably about the maximum scale jump you want to do between each era of training. (sources: src_031)

## The 1-Year vs 10-Year Estimation Rule
We always overestimate what can be done in a year and underestimate what can be done in 10 years. (sources: src_010)
