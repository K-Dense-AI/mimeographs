# Anti-Patterns Demis Hassabis Pushes Against

Demis Hassabis actively rejects several common paradigms in both the tech industry and traditional AI research, advocating instead for scientific rigor and generalized learning.

## Relying on Expert Systems (GOFAI)
Building AI using logic-based expert systems with massive amounts of hand-coded human priors. They are very brittle and cannot deal with the unexpected because they can only execute the heuristics human programmers have already figured out. The handcrafted human information often turns out to be wrong or too rigid, preventing the system from scaling and generalizing effectively. (sources: src_023, src_026, src_031)

## Move Fast and Break Things in AI
Applying the traditional Silicon Valley 'Move fast and break things' approach to AI development. Because AI is a uniquely powerful technology, breaking things in the real world could cause massive, irreversible damage. It requires a thoughtful, scientific approach instead, including consulting domain experts before releasing powerful models. (sources: src_026, src_038)

## Assuming Transformers Are Enough
Assuming that simply scaling up existing LLM architectures will automatically lead to AGI. Current paradigms lack continual learning, efficient memory, and long-term planning. Reaching AGI likely requires one or two more major, insightful innovations.
> "the transformer architecture... will underpin, be one of the main components of a future AGI system. But my prediction is it's not going to be enough on its own." (sources: src_019, src_027)

## Viewing AI Merely as Consumer Apps
Viewing AI merely as chatbots, consumer apps, or static search boxes. It misses the true potential of AI as a discovery engine that can accelerate human scientific breakthroughs and solve major challenges in medicine, energy, and the environment. (sources: src_020)

## Ignoring the Brain
Ignoring neuroscience in machine learning research. It discards the only working prototype of general intelligence we have, missing out on hugely important insights and general principles like memory replay or reinforcement learning.
> "It’s a mistake also to ignore the brain, which a lot of machine learning people do." (sources: src_033)

## Racing Without Coordination
Rushing AGI development purely through a ferocious commercial race without shared safety standards. Fragmentation makes it much harder to ensure resulting systems are technically safe. It prevents scientists from carefully and philosophically considering each step of what might be the most transformative technology in history.
> "if we can maybe it would be good to have a bit of slow a slightly slower pace than we're currently predicting even my timelines so that we can get this right society" (sources: src_016, src_014)

## Mathematical Reductionism in Biology
Attempting to boil down the workings of biology to a few mathematical equations. Biology is a phenomenally complex and emergent system, making it resistant to the simple mathematical descriptions that work well for physics. It requires AI as a description language instead. (sources: src_012)
