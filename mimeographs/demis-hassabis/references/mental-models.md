# Mental Models of Demis Hassabis

Demis Hassabis uses these mental models to contextualize the trajectory of AI, its relationship to human biology, and its potential to reshape the scientific landscape.

## Root Node Problems
Viewing human knowledge as a tree where solving foundational 'root node' challenges automatically unlocks entire new branches of downstream discovery. Instead of solving incremental issues, focus on fundamental problems like protein folding or nuclear fusion. Solving one root node unlocks pathways in disease understanding, drug design, and clean energy.
> "If you think of the tree of all knowledge. These are kind of root node problems which if you cracked it, it would unlock a whole branch of new research or new applications." (sources: src_019, src_016)

## Digital Biology
Viewing biology at its most fundamental level as a complex, emergent information processing system. This model allows researchers to reimagine biological discovery and drug development from first principles using computational models, effectively doing 'Science at Digital Speed' (e.g., using AlphaFold to predict 3D protein structures from 1D sequences).
> "Biology at its most fundamental level can be thought of as an information processing system albeit a phenomenally complex and emergent one" (sources: src_012, src_038)

## Capability Overhang
The concept that current AI models possess vast, untapped capabilities that even their creators do not fully understand. These latent capabilities are waiting to be unlocked through productization, deployment, and creative prompting. Users who immerse themselves in these tools can leapfrog in their professions by leveraging this overhang.
> "I think a kid these days could probably start a multi-billion dollar business in some ways using these tools in some new way that no one had thought about." (sources: src_027, src_014, src_016)

## 10x Industrial Revolution
A mental model for sizing the impact and speed of AGI by comparing it to the Industrial Revolution, but scaled up in magnitude and compressed in time. It helps contextualize the upcoming economic and societal upheaval, anticipating massive labor disruption while simultaneously expecting unprecedented advances in medicine and living standards.
> "I sometimes quantify like AGI at the coming of AGI as like 10 times the industrial revolution at 10 times the speed. So unfolding over a decade instead of a century." (sources: src_010)

## AI as a Telescope or Microscope
Viewing AI not as an oracle or deity, but as a human-made instrument that extends human perception to interrogate the fabric of reality. Just as the telescope allowed us to see the stars and the microscope allowed us to see cells, AI allows us to search huge spaces, model complex patterns, and test scientific ideas much faster than before.
(sources: src_020)

## Jagged Intelligences
A lens for understanding current AI capabilities: models exhibit extreme competence in specific areas but can fail at elementary tasks if the prompt is slightly altered. Unlike human general intelligence, which is more consistent, current AI models lack continual learning and robust world models, leading to a 'jagged' profile of extreme highs and inexplicable lows.
> "I sometimes call these systems jagged intelligences because they're really amazing at certain things when you pose the question in a certain way but in if you pose a question in a slightly different way they can actually still fail at quite elementary things." (sources: src_010)

## Better World Models Equal Less Search
The more accurate and rich a system's internal model of the world is, the less brute-force search it needs to make world-class decisions. AlphaZero looks at tens of thousands of positions compared to Deep Blue's millions, and human grandmasters look at only a few hundred, because humans and AlphaZero have much richer, more accurate models of the game.
(sources: src_031)
