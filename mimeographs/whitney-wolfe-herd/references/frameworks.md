This document details the step-by-step frameworks Whitney Wolfe Herd uses to evaluate business decisions and process emotional challenges.

## Emotional Acceptance Process
A method for processing hurt feelings, rejection, or the pain of others' opinions without letting it consume you.
1. Notice the hurt feelings without shaming yourself for having them.
2. Look directly at the feeling and acknowledge it objectively.
3. Identify the feeling as a wounded part of yourself.
4. Accept these pieces so the negative emotion doesn't take over your life.
> "I don't shame myself for having hurt feelings... I just kind of look at it and I stare at it and say, 'Okay, that's okay. That's like a wounded part of you...'"
(sources: src_012, src_016)

## The 'Why' Test for Business
A diagnostic to ensure a business or initiative is built on a sustainable, purpose-driven foundation.
When starting a business or facing severe setbacks, ask yourself: 'Who is this serving?' and 'Who is this helping?' Determine if the business solves a problem for the greater good of humanity. If the answer is only to make money for yourself, stop and reset your intention.
> "I always ask people what's the why like why are you doing it like what's what is the bigger what can this do for people like what is this solving"
(sources: src_012, src_032)

## Future-Self Intention Setting
A practical exercise for clarifying long-term dreams and goals when starting a new chapter in life.
1. Get an envelope, a piece of paper, and a pen.
2. Write your name and a date far in the future (e.g., 18 years from now).
3. Put yourself in that future present moment and write down what you have achieved, where you have gone, and how you have connected with yourself, others, and a higher power.
4. Seal the envelope and open it when you reach that target age.
> "I want you to write a letter to your future selves... put yourself in that present moment and tell yourself where you are. What have you achieved?"
(sources: src_010)
