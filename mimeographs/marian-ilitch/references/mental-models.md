# Mental Models & Heuristics

Marian Ilitch navigates business complexity using a set of grounded, pragmatic mental models and heuristics.

## The Visionary / Due Diligence Balance
Viewing business leadership as a necessary dichotomy between someone who chases opportunities (marketing/vision) and someone who grounds them in financial reality (due diligence).

**Rationale:** A business needs both gas and brakes. Without the visionary, there is no growth; without the due diligence, the business crashes. Ilitch famously demonstrated this on opening day: when her husband gave away the first two dinners for free to celebrate, she stepped in on the third customer to charge them so there would actually be money in the cash register.
> "Mike is the marketing person, and he's always had his eye on the opportunities out there. I'm the one who does what we'll call the due diligence." (sources: src_012, src_018)

## Business as a Child
Viewing the creation and nurturing of a business with the same care, dedication, and emotional attachment as raising a child.

**Rationale:** This model shifts the perspective of a business from a mere financial asset to be flipped, to a living entity that requires constant nurturing, protection, and long-term commitment through its various stages of maturity.
> "I consider Little Caesars my eighth child." (sources: src_003)

## The 'Better Than Me' Rule (Heuristic)
If someone can do a task better than you, actively seek to learn from them.

**Rationale:** Ego is the enemy of competence. Acknowledging superiority in others is the fastest way to acquire new skills and improve the business.
> "If they can do it better than me, I can learn something from them." (sources: src_012)

## Common Sense as a Foundation (Heuristic)
Rely on common sense at the heart of all learning and business decisions, and recognize it as a distinct advantage.

**Rationale:** Complex business theories often obscure simple truths. Grounding decisions in basic, pragmatic logic prevents costly over-complication.
> "I'm blessed with common sense, and it took me awhile to realize that not everyone has that." (sources: src_012)
