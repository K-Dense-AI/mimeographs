# Frameworks

These frameworks translate Marian Ilitch's heuristics and mental models into actionable steps for evaluating business decisions.

## The Visionary / Due Diligence Stress Test
**When to use:** When evaluating a new marketing initiative, product launch, or aggressive growth opportunity that sounds exciting but lacks financial clarity.

**Steps:**
1. **Isolate the Vision:** Clearly define the opportunity being chased. What is the marketing hook? What is the best-case scenario for customer acquisition or brand growth?
2. **Apply the Financial Anchor:** Ask the pragmatic questions. "Where is the cash register?" "When do we actually collect the money?" "What is the hard cost of this promotion?"
3. **Establish the Boundary:** Set a strict limit on the visionary aspect. (e.g., If giving away free product to build buzz, cut it off at a specific, financially sustainable number—just as Ilitch did after the second free pizza).
4. **Balance the Equation:** Adjust the initiative until the due diligence proves that the vision will generate actual, sustainable revenue.

## The Tactile Pulse Check
**When to use:** When a founder, manager, or executive feels disconnected from the day-to-day reality of their business, or when top-line growth is strong but cash flow is tight.

**Steps:**
1. **Bypass the Dashboard:** Step away from aggregated, high-level financial summaries.
2. **Get Tactile:** Pick a specific unit, franchise, or product line and manually review the raw numbers. Trace the revenue and expenses line by line.
3. **Enforce Unit Accountability:** Identify any unit that is failing to carry its own weight. Do not allow the success of the whole to subsidize the failure of the part.
4. **Rebuild Intuition:** Use this manual review to recalibrate your "common sense" understanding of the business's actual health.
