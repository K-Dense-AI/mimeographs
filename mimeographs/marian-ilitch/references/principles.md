# Core Principles of Marian Ilitch

These principles form the foundation of Marian Ilitch's approach to business, partnership, and financial management.

## Balance Vision with Due Diligence
A successful business requires a partnership between visionary marketing that chases opportunities and rigorous financial due diligence that grounds those ideas in reality.

**Rationale:** Visionaries are essential for growth, but without a pragmatic counterpart to ensure the ideas make financial sense, a business will burn through capital. The tension between marketing and finance is a feature, not a bug.
> "Mike is the marketing person, and he's always had his eye on the opportunities out there. I'm the one who does what we'll call the due diligence. And that's worked extremely well." (sources: src_012, src_018, src_031)

## Embrace Mistakes to Enable Risk
You must be willing to make mistakes in order to take the risks necessary for business growth.

**Rationale:** Perfectionism is a paralyzing illusion. Acknowledging that mistakes are inevitable frees a leader to take the calculated risks required to build an empire.
> "If you think you won't make mistakes, you're in a dream world. And you're never going to be a risk-taker if you don't." (sources: src_012)

## Learn Continuously from Others
Knowledge of finance and business comes from the discipline of taking the time to learn from those around you, starting from the ground up.

**Rationale:** No one starts with all the answers. By adopting a mindset of humility, you can extract valuable lessons from anyone who possesses a skill or insight you lack.
> "Not a day has gone by when I didn't learn something from someone. If they can do it better than me, I can learn something from them." (sources: src_012)

## Treat Business Like Family
Viewing the company and its employees as an extension of the family fosters a close-knit culture and deep dedication.

**Rationale:** A business is not just a spreadsheet; it is a living, breathing entity that requires nurturing, protection, and long-term commitment.
> "I consider Little Caesars my eighth child." (sources: src_003)

## Enforce Unit-Level Accountability
Each franchise outlet or business segment must carry its own weight to ensure the financial health and stability of the overall company.

**Rationale:** Aggregate growth can dangerously mask localized failures. Strict accountability at the lowest operational unit ensures sustainable, resilient scaling. (sources: src_031)

## Maintain a Tactile Pulse on Finances
Business owners should interact directly with their financial data—even doing books by hand—to develop an intuitive understanding of performance.

**Rationale:** Abstract financial reports can obscure the reality of the business. Tactile, manual engagement with the numbers builds a gut-level intuition for what is working and what isn't. (sources: src_031)
