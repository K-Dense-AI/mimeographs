# Mental Models of René Descartes

Descartes used vivid thought experiments and metaphors to force his mind out of its habitual reliance on the senses and into a state of pure rational inquiry. These models are tools for extreme skepticism and foundational thinking.

## The Evil Demon (Malicious Genius)
**Canonical statement:** An extreme thought experiment imagining an omnipotent, deceitful entity manipulating all perceptions and mathematical truths, used to test the absolute limits of skepticism.

**Rationale:** By imagining an all-powerful being actively manipulating all reality, the thinker is forced to suspend judgment on all external reality and isolate truths that are logically immune to any form of deception.

> "I will suppose, then, not that Deity, who is sovereignly good and the fountain of truth, but that some malignant demon, who is at once exceeding potent and deceitful, has employed all his artifice to deceive me"
*(sources: src_052, src_025, src_001, src_016, src_032, src_054)*

## The Architectural Metaphor of Knowledge
**Canonical statement:** Viewing knowledge as a physical structure. If the foundation is flawed, the entire structure must be demolished and rebuilt from scratch.

**Rationale:** Instead of dismantling the house stick by stick (belief by belief), you examine the foundation (basic principles). If the foundation is undermined, the entire structure of beliefs collapses automatically.

> "Once the foundations of a building are undermined, anything built on them collapses of its own accord. So, I will go straight to the basic principles on which all my former beliefs rested."
*(sources: src_050, src_048, src_016, src_001)*

## The Dream Argument
**Canonical statement:** A lens for evaluating the reliability of sensory experience by recognizing that vivid dreams perfectly mimic waking life, meaning current perceptions could be illusions.

**Rationale:** Because there are no definitive tests to prove you are awake, you cannot be absolutely certain that your current physical reality is real. Therefore, sensory data cannot be the foundation of absolute truth.

> "I perceive so clearly that there exist no certain marks by which the state of waking can ever be distinguished from sleep, that I feel greatly astonished"
*(sources: src_052, src_016)*

## The Barrel of Apples
**Canonical statement:** A metaphor for foundational epistemology: to ensure beliefs are sound, one must empty the mind completely and only put back the absolutely sound ones to prevent rot from spreading.

**Rationale:** To prevent a few rotten apples (false beliefs) from spoiling the whole barrel, one must tip out all the apples, inspect each one carefully, and only put the fresh, sound ones back in.
*(sources: src_000, src_025)*

## The Archimedean Point
**Canonical statement:** The concept of finding a single, firm, and immovable foundational truth upon which to build an entire system of knowledge.

**Rationale:** Just as Archimedes needed only one firm point to move the earth, a philosopher needs only one indubitable truth (the Cogito) to rebuild all knowledge after doubting everything else.
*(sources: src_052, src_000)*

## The Piece of Wax
**Canonical statement:** A lens to understand the true essence of physical matter. Sensory properties change, but the constant property of matter is 'extension' (occupying space).

**Rationale:** Observing a piece of wax before and after it melts by a fire. All its sensory qualities change, yet it remains the same wax, proving its essence is merely geometrical space occupancy, understood by the mind, not the senses.

> "the external world has an Essence too and that's simply extension it simply it takes up space that it's susceptible to being treated by geometry and the mathematical Sciences"
*(sources: src_025)*
