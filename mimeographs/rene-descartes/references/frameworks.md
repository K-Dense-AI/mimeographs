# Frameworks of René Descartes

Descartes did not just philosophize; he developed rigorous, step-by-step methodologies to ensure that his reasoning was free from error. These frameworks are designed to break down complexity and guarantee certainty.

## The Method of Radical Doubt
**Canonical statement:** A systematic process of discarding all beliefs that admit the slightest doubt to discover an indubitable foundational truth.

**Steps:**
1. Empty the mind of all current beliefs.
2. Reject sensory knowledge, as the senses occasionally deceive.
3. Entertain extreme skeptical hypotheses (like the Dream Argument or the Evil Demon) to test the limits of doubt.
4. Identify what remains absolutely impossible to doubt (the Cogito).

> "proceed by casting aside all that admits of the slightest doubt, not less than if I had discovered it to be absolutely false; and I will continue always in this track until I shall find something that is certain"
*(sources: src_052, src_039, src_000, src_025, src_001, src_016, src_009, src_054)*

## The Four Rules of Method
**Canonical statement:** A four-step process for seeking truth and resolving complex difficulties in the sciences.

**Steps:**
1. Never accept anything as true without evident knowledge of its truth.
2. Divide each difficulty into as many parts as possible.
3. Direct thoughts in an orderly manner, starting with the simplest objects and moving to the complex.
4. Make enumerations so complete that nothing is overlooked.

> "I thought that in place of the large number of rules that make up logic I would find the following four to be sufficient, provided that I made and kept to a strong resolution always to obey them."
*(sources: src_050, src_036)*

## Method of Analysis (Intuition and Deduction)
**Canonical statement:** Reducing complex propositions to simple ones, solving the simplest by direct intuition, and deducing the rest step-by-step.

**Steps:**
Withdraw the mind from empirical matters and sensory ideas. Grasp the necessary relations between innate ideas. Ascend through logical steps to deduce a knowledge of all the rest, ensuring no intermediate links are missed.

> "We shall be following this method exactly if we first reduce complicated and obscure propositions step by step to simpler ones, and then, starting with the intuition of the simplest ones of all, try to ascend through the same steps to a knowledge of all the rest."
*(sources: src_061, src_036, src_001)*

## Provisional Moral Code
**Canonical statement:** A practical guide to avoid indecision in daily life while questioning fundamental beliefs.

**Steps:**
1. Obey the laws and customs of your country.
2. Be firm and decisive in actions, following even doubtful opinions constantly once adopted.
3. Master yourself rather than fortune.
4. Devote your life to cultivating reason.

> "so as not to be indecisive in my actions during the time when reason obliged me to be so in my judgments, and in order to live as well as I could during this time, I formed for myself a provisional moral code"
*(sources: src_050)*
