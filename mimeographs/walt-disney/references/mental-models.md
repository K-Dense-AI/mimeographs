This document catalogs the mental models Walt Disney used to understand markets, manage talent, and design interconnected experiences.

## Media Synergy
Integrating different forms of media so they mutually support, fund, and promote one another.

Disney viewed secondary mediums (like television) not as standalone profit centers, but as loss-leading marketing engines to keep audiences aware of the primary product (motion pictures or theme parks). This creates a self-sustaining ecosystem.

> We went into TV with one thing in mind. Not to go out of the motion picture business, but to keep the audience aware of motion pictures.
(sources: src_019, src_051)

## The Pollinating Bee
A leadership model where the leader moves between different specialized teams to cross-pollinate ideas.

Instead of siloing departments, the leader collects small insights from story men, animators, and songwriters, spreading them around to elevate the entire project through diverse perspectives.

> an entire group of people get together story men and animators and songwriters and art background people and script writers all bet together in one room and talk about the project and he like to have everyone contribute their ideas
(sources: src_026)

## Character Integrity
Treating fictional characters as real entities with strict behavioral boundaries to ensure consistency and audience trust.

A creator must reject gags or artistic choices—even if they are funny in isolation—if they violate the established personality or physical rules of the character. This maintains the illusion of life and preserves the brand.
(sources: src_005)

## New Wine in Old Bottles
Taking established, classic art and reinterpreting it through a modern, innovative medium.

This is a lens for creative adaptation that breathes new life into old properties, making them accessible to contemporary, mainstream audiences despite the misgivings of traditionalists.

> Walt maintains that it is our function to put new wine in old bottles and that we are perfectly within our rights in interpreting this symphony in our own way
(sources: src_065)

## The Prototype Community
Treating urban development like industrial design by building a functional, perpetually evolving prototype.

Rather than theorizing about the future or patching old cities, you build a showcase on virgin land to actively test and demonstrate new solutions.

> It will be a community of tomorrow that will never be completed, but will always be introducing and testing and demonstrating new materials and systems.
(sources: src_001, src_022)
