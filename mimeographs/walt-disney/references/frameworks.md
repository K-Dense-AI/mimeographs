This document details the operational frameworks Walt Disney used to manage creative projects, scale his organization, and ensure his vision was executed accurately.

## Project Delegation Process
A phased approach to launching new creative projects while maintaining organizational scale.

To maintain the Disney standard without becoming a bottleneck, leadership must establish the DNA of a project early and then trust the team to execute.
1. Stay closely involved while chewing over the basic idea.
2. Set the pattern and standard for the project.
3. Let the staff take over execution.
4. Move on to coordinate other things.

> I'm always close to projects when we're chewing over the basic idea. Once the pattern is set, the answer to your question is simple. I let the staff take over and I go on to other things.
(sources: src_019)

## Storyboarding and Performance
A method for conceptualizing and communicating the vision for a complex narrative before committing resources to final production.

Animation and experience design require a shared understanding of flow and emotion. By visualizing the sequence and physically acting it out, the creator ensures the team understands the emotional beats before expensive production begins.
1. Create preliminary sketches of scenes and gags.
2. Pin the sketches sequentially to a large board to visualize narrative flow.
3. Gather the production staff and physically act out the entire story, performing the roles and movements to provide a living reference for the artists.
(sources: src_005, src_017)
