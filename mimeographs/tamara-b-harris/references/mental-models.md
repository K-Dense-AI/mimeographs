# Mental Models of Tamara B. Harris

These mental models illustrate how Tamara B. Harris conceptualizes the complex variables of aging, physical function, and epidemiological data.

## Composite Weight vs. Component Biology

**Canonical statement:** Viewing 'weight' not as a single monolithic variable, but as a composite of distinct tissues (lean mass, bone, and fat) that each contribute differently to health risks.

**Rationale:** Relying on overall weight traditionally causes confusion and controversy in geriatric epidemiology. Separating these components reveals the actual underlying biological processes, as fat mass, lean mass, and bone density have entirely different trajectories and impacts on mortality and mobility.

> "...the ability to separately examine lean mass, bone, and fat should shed light on the underlying biologic processes pertinent to risk."
*(sources: src_038, src_040)*

## Muscle Quality

**Canonical statement:** A metric for evaluating muscle health that looks beyond raw muscle mass by measuring the functional output (strength) per unit of regional muscle mass.

**Rationale:** Raw muscle mass can be highly misleading. For example, older adults with diabetes have greater overall muscle mass because they are bigger in body size, but their 'muscle quality' is significantly lower because their strength does not proportionally match their mass. Fat infiltration (muscle attenuation) further degrades this quality.

> "diabetes is associated with lower skeletal muscle strength and quality. These characteristics may contribute to the development of physical disability in older adults with diabetes."
*(sources: src_057)*

## Paradoxical Risk Factors

**Canonical statement:** The phenomenon where standard health risk factors flatten in risk or become protective in old age, while traditionally 'healthy' metrics become associated with higher mortality.

**Rationale:** In midlife, low weight and low blood pressure are healthy. In late life, the risk of death associated with a thinner BMI increases, while the risk associated with a heavier BMI flattens. This paradox is largely due to underlying frailty and reverse causation—the low weight is caused by an undiagnosed disease, rather than being a sign of health.

> "SPECIAL ISSUES IN OLD AGE: REVERSE CAUSATION (LOW BLOOD PRESSURE IS BAD FOR YOU AND HIGH BLOOR PRESSURE IS PROTECTIVE) WEIGHT, CHOLESTROL"
*(sources: src_049)*

## The Disablement Pathway

**Canonical statement:** A conceptual model tracking how weight-related conditions, age, and behavioral factors interact to cause body composition changes, leading to disability.

**Rationale:** Disability does not happen overnight. It follows a cascade from obesity or low physical activity, to changes in mid-thigh muscle cross-sectional area and fat infiltration, resulting eventually in an inability to perform basic functions like walking 400 meters.

> "Its central hypothesis was that body composition change is a common pathway through which weight-related health conditions and social and behavioral factors contribute to loss of independence in old age"
*(sources: src_059)*

## Interaction vs. Confounding

**Canonical statement:** A statistical lens for distinguishing between an exposure having different effects in different subgroups (interaction) versus a factor associated with both exposure and outcome (confounding).

**Rationale:** Interaction is about stratification. For example, low cholesterol has a different effect among frail individuals than among healthy individuals. Recognizing this prevents researchers from mistakenly treating a true biological interaction as mere statistical noise or confounding.

> "Interaction is all about stratification and occurs when an exposure has a different effect among different subgroups."
*(sources: src_049)*

## Allostatic Load & Cognitive Reserve

**Canonical statement:** Understanding how chronic stress from low socioeconomic status physically impacts the brain (allostatic load), and how quality education builds resilience against deterioration (cognitive reserve).

**Rationale:** Low socioeconomic status increases physiological stress, impairing neurogenesis. Conversely, education directly protects against brain deterioration by building cognitive reserve. Measuring literacy rather than just "years in school" provides a much more accurate proxy for this reserve.

> "Literacy, as a potential measure of quality of education, may affect cognition through many of the same mechanisms."
*(sources: src_054)*
