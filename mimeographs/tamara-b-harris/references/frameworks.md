# Frameworks of Tamara B. Harris

These frameworks operationalize Harris's principles into structured approaches for clinical assessment and epidemiological study design.

## Upstream Functional Assessment

**Canonical statement:** A framework for designing studies to detect early signs of functional decline in healthier older adults before overt disability occurs.

**Rationale:** Traditional measures of physical function focus on the lower end of capability (e.g., basic activities of daily living), which fails to capture the early onset of vulnerability in otherwise healthy adults. Moving the assessment upstream allows for earlier intervention.

**Steps:**
1. Recruit individuals currently free of self-reported functional limitations.
2. Administer challenging performance-based measures (e.g., 400m fast walk) to differentiate capacity levels.
3. Track performance times longitudinally to identify subclinical disease and vulnerability.

> "Conceptually, this moved the field of study upstream to discover the early signs of functional decline. Existing measures were focused on the lower end of functioning, so Health ABC developed new measures to discriminate among better-functioning adults."

## Aging-Prevention Paradigm

**Canonical statement:** A framework for tailoring clinical goals and interventions based on an older adult's current health status.

**Rationale:** Older adults are not a monolith. A preventive strategy that works for a healthy 70-year-old may be inappropriate or harmful for a frail 70-year-old. Interventions must be stratified by the patient's current position on the disablement pathway.

**Steps:**
1. Among the healthy: Focus interventions on preventing disease onset.
2. Among the 'at risk': Focus on stabilizing existing disease and preventing the onset of disability.
3. Among the frail: Focus on preventing the progression and severity of disability.

> "Aging-Prevention Paradigm
Among healthy prevent disease
Among ‘at risk’ stabilize disease, prevent disability
Among frail prevent progression of disability"
*(sources: src_049)*

## Biomarker Stratification Protocol

**Canonical statement:** Using inflammatory biomarkers to identify high-risk subgroups for targeted clinical interventions.

**Rationale:** Elevated inflammatory markers independently predict mortality even in healthy older adults. Using these markers allows clinicians to isolate individuals who are subclinically vulnerable and would benefit most from targeted treatments.

**Steps:**
1. Measure circulating levels of interleukin-6 (IL-6) and C-reactive protein (CRP) in healthy, nondisabled older adults.
2. Identify individuals with joint elevations of these markers.
3. Target this isolated high-risk subgroup for anti-inflammatory interventions.

> "These measures may be useful for identification of high-risk subgroups for anti-inflammatory interventions."
*(sources: src_020)*
