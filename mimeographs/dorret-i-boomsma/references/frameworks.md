# Frameworks of Dorret I. Boomsma

These frameworks represent the methodological structures Boomsma uses to design studies, partition variance, and isolate biological mechanisms.

## Extended Twin-Family Design

Expanding beyond MZ/DZ pairs to include parents, siblings, spouses, and offspring to test assumptions of the classical twin method and estimate complex variance.

**Steps:**
1. Recruit a large cohort of twins.
2. Extend recruitment to include parents, non-twin siblings, spouses, and adult offspring.
3. Collect longitudinal phenotype and genotype data.
4. Model the data simultaneously to correct for dependencies, test for a "special twin environment," and estimate non-additive genetic factors.

> "The NTR combines the classical twin design with extended and more complex family designs to genetic epidemiology and heritability studies."
*(sources: src_019, src_046, src_048, src_057)*

## Monozygotic Discordant (MZD) Design

Using identical twins who differ on a specific trait to perfectly control for genetic background while isolating environmental pathways.

**Steps:**
1. Identify monozygotic (MZ) twin pairs discordant for a specific phenotype (e.g., depression or smoking).
2. Conduct deep phenotyping (e.g., fMRI, clinical assessments) on both twins.
3. Analyze intra-pair differences to isolate environmental factors, as the genetic sequence is identical.

> "The MZ discordant (MZD) design is a powerful method to look at environmental pathways that mediate differences within MZ pairs."
*(sources: src_019, src_057)*

## The Classical Twin Design

Comparing concordance rates between MZ and DZ twins to estimate the relative role of genetic versus environmental factors.

**Steps:**
1. Identify cohorts of MZ and DZ twins.
2. Measure the trait of interest in both twins.
3. Calculate correlations for MZ and DZ pairs.
4. Compare resemblance, expecting MZ correlation to equal h^2 + c^2, and DZ correlation to equal 1/2 h^2 + c^2.
5. Estimate heritability by doubling the difference between the MZ and DZ correlations.

> "These measures are calculated for MZ and DZ pairs, and the comparison between the two values will suggest a genetic effect when MZ twin pairs show a higher resemblance than DZ twin pairs."
*(sources: src_008)*

## Structural Equation Modelling (SEM) for Twin Data

Modeling genotypic and environmental effects as latent variables to handle multivariate phenotypes, opposite-sex twins, and missing data.

**Steps:**
1. Model genotypic and environmental effects as unmeasured (latent) variables.
2. Estimate contributions as regression coefficients on observed variables.
3. Use raw data likelihood estimators to handle missing data gracefully.
4. Simultaneously analyze data from male, female, and opposite-sex twins to map complex architectures.

> "STRUCTURAL EQUATION MODELLING (SEM), also known as covariance modelling, is a more general alternative approach, in which genotypic and environmental effects are modelled as the contribution of unmeasured (latent) variables..."
*(sources: src_046)*

## Retrospective Epigenetic Profiling

Using genome-wide methylation arrays to determine if a subject originally began development as a monozygotic twin.

**Steps:**
1. Extract DNA from the subject.
2. Run genome-wide methylation arrays to capture the epigenetic profile.
3. Compare the resulting methylation pattern against the established, unique monozygotic twin epigenetic signature.

> "The signature is almost strong enough, certainly for research purposes, to retrospectively ask the question whether a patient with a particular congenital disorder might have started life as a monocycotic twin."
*(sources: src_011)*
