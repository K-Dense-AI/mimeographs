# Daphne Koller's Core Principles

This document outlines the foundational principles that guide Daphne Koller's approach to machine learning, biology, and organizational design.

## True innovation happens at the boundaries of disciplines
The most transformative solutions emerge when distinct fields intersect, provided domain experts and technologists treat each other as equal collaborators. If kept separate, ML scientists will solve biologically irrelevant problems, and biologists will only ask ML to automate boring tasks. Together, they discover the right questions to ask.
> "what you get if you put them in a room together and actually get to the point where they communicate with each other effectively, is that not only do you get better solutions, you get better problems."
*(sources: src_006, src_034, src_000)*

## Deliberate Cross-Disciplinary Architecture
The most impactful work happens at the boundaries of disciplines, but this requires deliberately building a shared vocabulary and culture. Life scientists and computational scientists naturally use different jargon. If you don't deliberately build a culture where they form a single team, they will talk past each other.
> "if you create a world in which those people are actually part of a team they develop a shared vocabulary and a shared Vision magic just happens"
*(sources: src_014, src_022)*

## Drug failures primarily stem from pursuing the wrong biological target
Most drugs fail in late-stage clinical development not because the molecule was poorly designed, but because the biological target was incorrect for the specific disease and patient population. AI's greatest potential lies in identifying the right therapeutic targets rather than just generating molecules.
> "most of the things that fail in clinical development is because our therapeutic hypothesis was just wrong we don't understand the biology"
*(sources: src_012, src_000)*

## Generate Fit-for-Purpose Data
Data is not fungible. To solve complex physical problems like human biology, you cannot rely on existing web-scale data; you must intentionally generate massive, high-quality, domain-specific data. Existing data collected from the web does not contain the answers needed to disentangle complex biology.
> "We need to generate the right data—data that is fit-for-purpose for machine learning."
*(sources: src_014, src_048)*

## Maximize your unique value and leverage
Focus on problems where your specific skills, experience, and mindset allow you to have a disproportionately large impact compared to the next best person. When choosing what to work on, consider not just the absolute value of the work, but the value you bring relative to what would happen if someone else took your place.
> "i've always looked for opportunities where the experience the skills the the mindset that i bring have a disproportionate leverage i'm a big believer in leverage"
*(sources: src_006, src_034)*

## Technology transfer requires moving people, not just publishing papers
To make a real-world impact, you must build products. You cannot simply publish a paper and expect industry to adopt it without the original creators championing it. Real change requires the people who developed the ideas to transition with them into practice.
> "we realize that you can't really do Tech transfer with a paper you can do Tech transfer with a product"
*(sources: src_012, src_034)*

## The privileged have a moral imperative to improve the world
Those born into affluent, well-educated families without basic struggles have a higher burden to give back and leave the world better than they found it.
> "For those of us who had the privilege of being born to relatively affluent, well-educated families, where we didn’t need to struggle for where our next meal is coming from, that burden is actually even higher and we — we should be thinking about how we can give back."
*(sources: src_006, src_027)*

## AI Amplifies Rigorous Science
In the physical world, AI is an amplifier of rigorous scientific experimentation, not a substitute for it. While AI can move at the speed of computation in the virtual world, the physical world is constrained by data scarcity and the need for causal validation.
> "Done right, A.I. is an amplifier of rigorous biology—not a substitute for it."
*(sources: src_048)*

## Anti-Hypothesis Driven Discovery
Human biology is too complex for the human brain to comprehend manually. Instead of starting with human-generated hypotheses, collect massive amounts of unbiased data and let machine learning surface the insights.
> "We take a very different approach that is anti-hypothesis driven... rather than starting out with a hypothesis and then doing experiments to test those out we do experiments to feed into machine learning models who tell us what the interesting findings the interesting insights that emerge from those data"
*(sources: src_022)*

## Causality for Physical Interventions
While correlational data is sufficient for observational tasks, intervening in complex physical systems requires causal understanding. Tasks like text summarization can rely on correlation, but treating a disease requires knowing what happens when you intervene.
*(sources: src_014)*

## Causality is necessary for drug development, but mechanism is not
Knowing that perturbing a specific target causes a desired phenotypic effect is enough to develop a drug, even if the exact biological mechanism remains unknown.
> "understanding cause and effect is absolutely necessary for drug development understanding the entirety of the why is... neither necessary or sufficient"
*(sources: src_011)*

## Industry Incentives Drive Usable Products
To build persistent, reusable products that have real-world impact, one must align with industry incentives rather than academic ones. Academia incentivizes publishing papers, not building persistent data pipelines.
*(sources: src_014)*

## Machine learning is the mathematical foundation for biology
Unlike physics, biology is an evolved, highly complex natural system that cannot be captured by de novo mathematical models. Machine learning is required to let the data speak.
> "math is to physics as machine learning is to biology."
*(sources: src_000)*

## Measurement is the prerequisite to understanding biological systems
You cannot design effective therapeutic interventions without first mapping the causal pathways of a disease, which requires high-fidelity, quantitative data.
*(sources: src_012)*

## Pragmatism Over Elegance
Prioritize methods that solve the real problem over the coolest or most elegant machine learning method. Solving a real problem provides a clarifying lens that strips away unnecessary complexity.
> "First of all, the focus of the work is not do you use the coolest, most elegant machine learning method, it's do you use the thing that works."
*(sources: src_014)*

## Stick to your principles over short-term gains
Making principled decisions might be slower or face internal pushback, but it builds long-term success and provides a defensible moral compass.
> "i think sticking to your principles really allows you to when there is controversy to go back and say look our you know don't judge us by our words justice by our actions"
*(sources: src_006)*
