# Daphne Koller's Frameworks

This document details the step-by-step frameworks Daphne Koller uses to approach complex problems in science, business, and career planning.

## Interdisciplinary Dataset Design
A method for applying machine learning to a new scientific domain by collaboratively designing datasets.
1. Put domain scientists and machine learning experts in a room together as equal partners.
2. Ask the domain experts to identify the really big questions they wish they had a magic wand to solve.
3. Evaluate if machine learning is actually the right tool for those specific questions.
4. Collaboratively design experiments and datasets specifically to allow ML approaches to be trained and applied effectively.
*(sources: src_034, src_027)*

## The Three Buckets of Drug Discovery
A framework for mapping the drug discovery and development process to identify where AI and resources should be allocated.
1. **Biology discovery:** Discovering new therapeutic hypotheses (identifying which target to modulate).
2. **Molecule generation:** Turning the therapeutic hypothesis into an actual molecule with the right properties.
3. **Clinical development:** Enabling and accelerating the clinical trial process.
*(sources: src_012, src_000)*

## A.I.-First End-to-End Drug Discovery
A pipeline for discovering entirely new treatments by re-architecting the drug discovery process around machine learning.
1. Generate fit-for-purpose data to disentangle biology.
2. Use ML for novel target discovery.
3. Advance to drug discovery to design molecules against these new targets.
4. Move into the clinic, turning an artisanal journey into a repeatable process.
> "We have built an A.I.-first engine that transforms drug discovery and development end-to-end—from target discovery, through drug discovery, and into the clinic."
*(sources: src_048)*

## Bridging Data Modalities for Causal Predictions
A method for building predictive models of causal interventions in humans by linking in-vitro cellular data with human clinical data.
1. Collect human genetic and phenotypic data to establish ground truth causality.
2. Generate in-house cellular data using genetic interventions.
3. Use ML to create representations of both modalities.
4. Bridge the modalities so lab experiments predict human interventions.
> "we've taken a rather unusual strategy of bringing together and bridging between two very distinct and complementary data modalities"
*(sources: src_022)*

## Decision-Making for Maximum Impact
A framework for deciding which problems to dedicate your life to during major career transitions.
1. Identify a deep internal urgency to do something meaningful that touches people's lives.
2. Evaluate your unique abilities, experiences, and mindset.
3. Look for opportunities where your specific background provides disproportionate leverage.
4. Choose the path where you can do the work much better than the next best person.
> "if people are facing decision points i would encourage them to think about what do you bring to the table that is unique to the point that you can do something much better than the next best person"
*(sources: src_006)*

## Intervening on Meeting Microaggressions
A tactical approach for allies to redirect credit when an underrepresented person's idea is co-opted.
1. Notice the dynamic where the original speaker was ignored.
2. Use your voice to speak up, especially if you are in a position of privilege.
3. Validate the second speaker's contribution while explicitly redirecting credit back to the original speaker (e.g., 'Yeah John, that was a great idea and Jane made a very similar point a few moments ago').
> "it's okay for you to say yeah john that was a great idea and jane made that it's a very similar point a few moments ago and i think that is very very impactful"
*(sources: src_006)*

## Probabilistic Graphical Models (PGMs)
A structural taxonomy for building automated systems that need to reason under uncertainty.
1. **Representation:** Construct an interpretable model of the complex system.
2. **Inference:** Manipulate the model using reasoning algorithms to reach conclusions.
3. **Learning:** Automatically learn or refine the models from data when manual construction is difficult.
> "For each class of models, the text describes the three fundamental cornerstones: representation, inference, and learning, presenting both basic concepts and advanced techniques."
*(sources: src_043)*

## The Data Printing Factory
A system for generating massive, high-quality biological data to train high-capacity AI models when existing data is insufficient.
1. Generate cellular systems from human-derived cells at massive scale using robotics to avoid human batch effects.
2. Perturb the cells (e.g., via CRISPR) to generate causal data.
3. Measure resulting cell states across multiple modalities (omics, imaging).
4. Integrate cellular data with human-level clinical data.
*(sources: src_014)*
