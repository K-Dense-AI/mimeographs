# Frameworks of Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw

These frameworks represent Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw's structured approaches to innovation, scaling, and market strategy.

## Four Buckets of Innovation
A portfolio management framework for balancing R&D investments across different risk profiles.
1. Invest small amounts in "experimental innovation" (high risk).
2. Invest more heavily in "incremental" and "evolutionary innovation" (lower risk).
3. Scale up investments significantly when experimental innovations reach the "breakthrough" phase.
4. Periodically unlock the pipeline to create value.
> "We start with small investments in experimental innovation which we realise will require large investments when it comes to the breakthrough phase..." (sources: src_045)

## Phases of Entrepreneurial Evolution
A timeline for building and scaling a technology-driven enterprise over decades.
1. **Survival/Learning Phase (Years 1-10):** Focus on learning and commercializing core technologies.
2. **Leveraging Phase (Years 11-20):** Leverage proven technologies to build scale.
3. **Transformational Phase:** Use critical mass to change the business model entirely.
4. **Brand Equity Phase:** Build brand equity on the transformed foundation.
> "In the first 10 years, my learning phase, I was very focused on learning the technology... The next 10 years... were about leveraging those technologies and building scale..." (sources: src_045)

## Affordable Cancer Care Model
A framework for designing healthcare solutions in developing countries to prevent financial ruin for patients.
1. Leverage economies of scale to reduce per-patient costs.
2. Focus heavily on early-stage diagnosis to make treatments less resource-intensive.
3. Combine therapeutic and diagnostic innovations for personalized medicine.
> "The affordable cancer care model relies on economics of scale and early stage diagnosis." (sources: src_047)

## Competitive Differentiation Strategy
A strategic approach to entering markets by avoiding crowded spaces and embracing risk.
1. Understand the competitive landscape deeply.
2. Identify high-risk, high-reward opportunities where fewer people are willing to compete.
3. Build a niche in that space to establish a hard-to-overtake leadership position.
> "I have focused on understanding the competitive landscape of my market very well – and then building a niche, where we can be in a leadership position that is hard to overtake." (sources: src_019)

## Modular Business Strategy
A funding and growth strategy for industries with long gestation periods, like biotech.
1. Adopt a global market focus from the beginning.
2. Structure the business to make money at every phase of development.
3. Reinvest the profits from each phase back into the business to sustain long-term growth.
*(sources: src_028)*

## BioE3 Framework
A national vision for structuring large-scale strategic investments in biotechnology.
1. Integrate the bioeconomy, bioenvironment, and bioemployment into a unified vision.
2. Scale advanced biomanufacturing capabilities.
3. Position the ecosystem as a global powerhouse for specific verticals like biosimilars and synthetic biology.
> "The path forward hinges on accelerating the BioE3 framework — a national vision that integrates bioeconomy, bioenvironment, and bioemployment." (sources: src_035)

## Raising Capital for Novel Ideas
A structured approach to pitching risky or unconventional business ideas to investors.
1. Articulate the idea clearly.
2. Dimension the value and market opportunity.
3. Demonstrate differentiation and unmet needs.
4. Find investors who share the vision.
5. Build a track record of delivering on promises.
> "If you can articulate the value of your idea... and how you're basically trying to make a difference... then I think people understand what you're trying to do and will invest in you." (sources: src_008)
