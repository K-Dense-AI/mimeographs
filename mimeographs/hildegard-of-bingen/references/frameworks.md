This document outlines Hildegard of Bingen's structured methods for processing inspiration, treating ailments, and correcting behavior.

## Visionary Reception and Transcription
A method for receiving, interpreting, and documenting divine revelations exactly as they are experienced in a waking state.

Steps:
1. Experience the "reflection of the living light" in a fully waking state, avoiding ecstatic trances.
2. Hold the visual and auditory elements of the vision in memory.
3. Simultaneously see, hear, and understand the meaning of the vision.
4. Write down the exact, unpolished words heard in the vision without adding philosophical embellishment.

> "And the words which I write I have seen and heard in the vision; nor do I put down words other than those I hear in the vision, and I present them in Latin, unpolished, just as I hear them in the vision."
(sources: src_045)

## Untwisting Vice
A spiritual framework for addressing destructive behavior by recognizing vice as a twisted form of virtue and working to untwist it back into its healthy form, rather than simply banning it.

Steps:
1. Recognize that the vice is not an independent evil, but a twisted form of a virtue.
2. Resist the urge to simply ban, punish, or suppress the vice.
3. Identify the original virtue and work to untwist the vice back into its right, healthy form.

> "a vice being a twisted form of a virtue so if you see a vice you don't ban it you say how do I untwist it into its right form"
(sources: src_012)

## Humoral Temperature and Moisture Treatment
A medical framework for diagnosing and treating physical ailments by classifying conditions and cures based on temperature and moisture (hot, cold, wet, dry).

Steps:
1. Classify the patient's condition in terms of temperature and moisture.
2. Select a material cure (herbs, foods, gemstones) classified by the same system.
3. Apply the treatment to either counter the condition's temperature with an opposing one, or use a similar temperature based on the theory that "like calls to like."

> "Treatment would either counter a condition’s temperature with an opposing one or use a similar temperature according to the theory that like calls to like."
(sources: src_035)
