This document details the structured frameworks Frank B. Hu uses to analyze diet quality, population health, and environmental sustainability.

## Dietary Pattern Factor Analysis
A methodological framework for deriving overall dietary patterns from food-frequency questionnaires to study diet-disease relations. It involves collecting dietary data, collapsing items into predefined food groups, using principal component factor analysis to derive patterns, rotating factors for interpretability, and labeling them based on empirical criteria.
> "Factor analysis (principal component) was used to derive food patterns based on the 40 food groups... The factors were rotated by an orthogonal transformation... to achieve simpler structure with greater interpretability."
(sources: src_031, src_036)

## EAT-Lancet Commission Systems Approach
A global framework for developing dietary guidelines that balance human health with environmental sustainability. The steps are: define a healthy reference diet, define planetary boundaries (GHG emissions, land/water use), apply global systems modeling to analyze measures that stay within boundaries, and recommend international strategies.
> "The first step of this committee did is to define what is a healthy diet... the second step is to define planetary boundaries... and the surf step is to apply global systems modeling framework"
(sources: src_005)

## Precision Nutrition & Systems Epidemiology
A framework for moving beyond 'one size fits all' by incorporating systems biology tools into epidemiological studies. It involves analyzing an individual's biology (microbiome, metabolomics), assessing cultural and lifestyle factors, tailoring specific dietary interventions, and monitoring compliance using objective biomarkers.
> "Dietary recommendations need to be tailored to individuals biology or physiology based on microbiome analysis metabolomics profiling and also dietary recommendations need to tailored to individuals cultural background and lifestyle factors."
(sources: src_009, src_011)

## Global Diet Quality Score (GDQS)
A framework for measuring and comparing diet quality globally across diverse food systems without relying on complex nutrient composition data. It uses an entirely food-based scoring system to predict nutrient adequacy and non-communicable disease risk.
> "We developed a Global Diet Quality Score (GDQS) that is: Entirely food-based (no nutrients involved in scoring)... Uses a modestly expanded food list relative to other metrics..."
(sources: src_022)

## 5 Low-Risk Lifestyle Factors for Longevity
A framework for assessing lifestyle behaviors to maximize disease-free life expectancy. The factors are: 1. Never smoke. 2. ≥3.5 hours/week moderate to vigorous physical activity. 3. High diet quality. 4. Moderate alcohol intake. 5. Normal body weight (BMI < 25).
> "Low-risk lifestyle factors included: cigarette smoking (never smoking), physically active... high diet quality... moderate alcohol intake... and normal weight."
(sources: src_034)
