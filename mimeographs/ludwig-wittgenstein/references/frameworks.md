This document details the frameworks Ludwig Wittgenstein used to dismantle conceptual confusion and clarify thought.

## Philosophical Therapy
A method for dissolving philosophical perplexity by examining how language is actually used, rather than building abstract theories. When feeling trapped by a conceptual problem, recognize it as linguistic confusion. Resist the urge to theorize. Instead, 'look and see' how the problematic words are used in ordinary language. Marshall reminders and examples of various language-games to break the illusion of a single, essential meaning, thereby showing 'the fly the way out of the fly-bottle.'
> "The work of the philosopher consists in marshalling reminders for a particular purpose."
*(sources: src_004, src_017)*

## Logical Clarification of Thoughts
An analytical approach to identifying nonsense in traditional philosophical musings by translating expressions into strict logical symbolism. Recognize that most philosophical questions arise from misunderstanding the logic of language. Translate expressions into a logical symbolism that obeys logical grammar. Identify what can be clearly said (propositions of natural science) versus what can only be shown, and state the senselessness of questions that attempt to speak about the unspeakable.
> "The object of philosophy is the logical clarification of thoughts."
*(sources: src_005, src_025)*

## Language-Games Analysis
A framework for dispelling philosophical fog by studying language in primitive, highly circumscribed applications. Imagine a primitive use of language (e.g., a builder and an assistant using only 'block', 'pillar', 'slab', 'beam'). Observe how the words are taught through training and practice. Analyze the specific role of the words within the actions they are woven into. Use this simplified model to command a clear view of complex language use.
> "It disperses the fog to study the phenomena of language in primitive kinds of application in which one can command a clear view of the aim and functioning of the words."
*(sources: src_040)*
