# Frameworks of Ronald C. Kessler

These frameworks provide step-by-step methodologies for designing precision treatment research and allocating clinical resources.

## Four Requirements for Precision Treatment Research
**Canonical statement:** A methodological framework for designing studies intended to match mental health patients to their optimal treatments.
**Rationale:** Traditional clinical trials fail to produce actionable precision medicine insights because they are too small, too narrow, and lack baseline depth. This framework corrects those deficiencies.
**Steps:**
1. Gather comprehensive predictor sets, including long baseline assessments and non-traditional data.
2. Make comprehensive comparisons across the full range of available treatments, not just two-arm comparisons.
3. Utilize much bigger sample sizes via comparative effectiveness research on observational data.
4. Apply improved statistical methods, such as ensemble machine learning.
> "The what we need to do to move forward in this line of work is four things... comprehensive predictor sets... comprehensive comparisons across the full range of available treatments... much much bigger sample sizes... improved statistical methods"

## Precision Treatment Modeling
**Canonical statement:** A three-step approach to allocating limited healthcare resources and optimizing treatment selection.
**Rationale:** Healthcare systems have limited resources and must triage patients effectively. This framework separates the prediction of risk from the prediction of treatment response, then combines them for optimal resource allocation.
**Steps:**
1. Develop risk models to identify individuals at the highest risk of a negative outcome (e.g., suicide) for targeted intensive interventions.
2. Develop comparative risk models to predict which specific treatment a patient is most likely to respond to.
3. Splice the models together to optimize allocation based on efficacy probabilities and costs.
> "trying to first of all when we don't have comparative treatments we just have doing something versus doing nothing who are the people at risk and then in case is where we have multiple treatments trying to uh figure out models to say how do you get the right treatment to the right person"
*(sources: src_014)*
