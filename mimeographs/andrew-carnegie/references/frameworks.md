This document details the step-by-step frameworks Andrew Carnegie used to structure his philanthropy and business operations.

## The Gospel of Wealth (Duty of the Wealthy)
**Canonical statement:** A framework for how a successful individual should live and dispose of their accumulated fortune.
**Rationale:** Wealth is a public trust, and the individual who accumulated it is best positioned to distribute it effectively during their lifetime.
**Steps:**
1. Set an example of modest, unostentatious living, shunning display or extravagance.
2. Provide moderately for the legitimate wants of dependents.
3. Treat all remaining surplus revenues as trust funds.
4. Administer these trust funds during your lifetime to produce the most beneficial results for the community.
> "This, then, is held to be the duty of the man of wealth: To set an example of modest, unostentatious living... and, after doing so, to consider all surplus revenues which come to him simply as trust funds..."

## Community-Matched Philanthropy
**Canonical statement:** A structured approach to funding public institutions to ensure long-term viability and community investment.
**Rationale:** Institutions survive and thrive only when the local community has a vested financial interest in their ongoing success.
**Steps:**
1. The wealthy benefactor provides the upfront capital required to build the physical infrastructure (e.g., a library building).
2. Require the local government or community to formally agree to fund ongoing maintenance and operations (often a 10% annual match).
3. Integrate the new institution into the city's existing public property and services.
> "...provided the community will accept and maintain it as a public institution, as much a part of the city property as its public schools..."
*(sources: src_008, src_036, src_052)*

## Cost-Centric Market Domination
**Canonical statement:** A strategy for operating in a commodity or manufacturing business subject to cyclical prices.
**Rationale:** Market prices are outside of a company's control, but operational efficiency and unit economics can be permanently optimized to ensure survival.
**Steps:**
1. Cut the prices to scoop the market.
2. Watch the costs strictly down to the penny.
3. Allow the profits to take care of themselves.
> "Cut the prices, scoop the market, watch the costs, and the profits will take care of themselves."
*(sources: src_024)*
