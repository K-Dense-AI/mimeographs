This document outlines the core principles that governed Andrew Carnegie's approach to business, wealth, and philanthropy.

## Distribute Wealth During Your Lifetime
**Canonical statement:** The wealthy have a moral obligation to actively distribute their fortunes for the public good while they are still alive, rather than hoarding it until death.
**Rationale:** Surplus wealth should be viewed as a trust fund. Leaving it to the state or to heirs represents a failure of duty. Actively administering it bridges the gap between the rich and the poor and brings peace to society.
> "The man who dies thus rich dies disgraced."

## Empower Self-Improvement Over Direct Charity
**Canonical statement:** Philanthropy should focus on providing the means for people to improve themselves, rather than giving direct handouts that encourage dependency.
**Rationale:** Providing infrastructure like libraries, universities, and parks acts as a ladder for those who desire to rise. Direct almsgiving degrades the individual and rewards vice or sloth.
> "In bestowing charity, the main consideration should be to help those who will help themselves; to provide part of the means by which those who desire to improve may do so"
*(sources: src_000, src_004, src_008, src_010, src_030, src_033, src_036, src_048, src_052)*

## Libraries are the Ultimate Philanthropic Investment
**Canonical statement:** Funding free public libraries is the most productive use of philanthropic money because it provides ambitious individuals with the tools to educate themselves.
**Rationale:** Access to books instills a love of reading, which provides lifelong instruction and enjoyment. This holds far more enduring value for a working person than a temporary financial gift or direct wage increase.
> "A library outranks any other one thing a community can do to benefit its people. It is a never failing spring in the desert."
*(sources: src_008, src_022, src_031, src_033, src_044, src_058)*

## Watch the Costs and Profits Will Follow
**Canonical statement:** Strictly controlling operational costs down to the penny ensures long-term profitability and resilience against market fluctuations.
**Rationale:** Profits and prices are cyclical and subject to transient market forces, but savings achieved in the cost of goods are permanent. Operational efficiency positions a company to survive downturns and acquire competitors.
> "Cut the prices, scoop the market, watch the costs, and the profits will take care of themselves."
*(sources: src_016, src_024, src_031)*

## Organize Smarter People
**Canonical statement:** Success comes not from possessing all technical knowledge yourself, but from the ability to organize and manage experts who know more than you do.
**Rationale:** Understanding human nature and knowing how to fit gifted managers into an overall system is worth more than the physical assets or machinery of a business.
> "A success not to be attributed to what I have known or done myself, but to the faculty of knowing and choosing others who did know better than myself."
*(sources: src_016, src_024, src_031)*

## Do Not Spoil Heirs with Wealth
**Canonical statement:** Leaving vast fortunes to children is harmful to their character and acts as a burden rather than a blessing.
**Rationale:** Inherited wealth ruins work ethic and is usually driven by misguided family pride rather than the actual welfare of the child or the state.
> "I should as soon leave to my son a curse as the almighty dollar."
*(sources: src_004, src_036, src_052)*

## Inequality and Capital Concentration are Beneficial
**Canonical statement:** Great inequality of wealth and the concentration of capital are essential for the progress of the human race and the refinement of civilization.
**Rationale:** The accumulation of wealth by capable individuals drives down the cost of commodities, turning former luxuries into necessities. It is a natural evolutionary step from a disorganized state to an organized one.
> "Much better this great irregularity than universal squalor."
*(sources: src_003, src_004, src_048)*

## Cultivate an Internal Scorecard
**Canonical statement:** The most important judge of your life and actions is your own conscience, which cannot be deceived.
**Rationale:** Rely on your own internal judgment rather than external validation or the sermons of others to guide moral and business decisions.
> "The judge within sits in the Supreme Court and can never be cheated hence the grand rule of Life your own reproach alone do fear."
*(sources: src_024, src_031)*

## Maintain Default Optimism
**Canonical statement:** A sunny disposition and the ability to laugh through life's troubles is incredibly valuable and can be actively cultivated.
**Rationale:** The mind, like the body, can be moved from the shade into the sunshine. This resilience is worth more than a fortune.
> "A sunny disposition is worth more than a fortune."
*(sources: src_024, src_031)*

## Concentrate Capital and Attention
**Canonical statement:** Enormous dividends come from investing deeply in a single business rather than spreading your focus and resources.
**Rationale:** Put every dollar of your capital and all of your time into one line of business and watch it closely.
> "My advice to young men would be not only to concentrate their whole time and attention on the one business in life in which they engage, but to put every dollar of their capital into it."
*(sources: src_024)*
