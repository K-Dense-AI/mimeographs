This document captures the cognitive lenses and metaphors Andrew Carnegie used to understand society, industry, and human motivation.

## The Millionaire as Trustee
**Canonical statement:** Viewing the wealthy individual not as the absolute owner of their surplus, but as a temporary steward or agent holding funds for the public good.
**Rationale:** The trustee leverages their superior business acumen and organizational experience to distribute wealth more effectively than if it were scattered in trifling amounts among the masses.
> "...the millionaire will be but a trustee for the poor, intrusted for a season with a great part of the increased wealth of the community, but administering it for the community far better than it could or would have done for itself."
*(sources: src_000, src_004, src_030, src_048, src_052)*

## Ladders for the Aspiring (Hand Upward vs Handout)
**Canonical statement:** A lens for evaluating philanthropic investments by prioritizing infrastructure that enables ambitious individuals to climb out of poverty.
**Rationale:** Instead of giving direct handouts, wealth should be used to build parks, works of art, and public institutions like libraries that people must actively use to benefit from.
> "Place within the community's reach the ladders upon which the aspiring can rise."
*(sources: src_004, src_010, src_036)*

## The Judge Within
**Canonical statement:** Viewing one's conscience as an internal Supreme Court that cannot be cheated, serving as the ultimate arbiter of one's actions.
**Rationale:** You evaluate your life story and actions based on your own internal scorecard rather than external validation or public opinion.
> "The judge within sits in the Supreme Court and can never be cheated hence the grand rule of Life your own reproach alone do fear."
*(sources: src_024, src_031)*

## Business as a Giant Machine
**Canonical statement:** Treating the complex process of manufacturing and corporate organization as a giant machine where both human and mechanical parts must be constantly retooled and optimized.
**Rationale:** This allows a leader to organize experts and workers to fit into the overall objective without needing to understand the granular technical details of the machinery themselves.
*(sources: src_024, src_031)*

## The Three-Legged Stool of Industry
**Canonical statement:** A metaphor viewing the industrial world as an alliance of three equal, interdependent forces: labor, capital, and ability (management).
**Rationale:** If any one leg is missing or unequal, the enterprise cannot stand. Workers, money, and skill are all equally necessary.
> "Labor, capital, and ability are a three-legged stool... They are equal members of the great triple alliance which moves the industrial world."
*(sources: src_022)*

## The Fishing Sloop (Hub and Spoke Model)
**Canonical statement:** A metaphor for system design: a massive central hub is useless to the masses without a network of local branches to distribute its value.
**Rationale:** Carnegie argued against building a grand central library unless it was connected to neighborhood branch systems, ensuring it was accessible to everyday people.
> "A great central public library without branch libraries in a large city resembles a fishing sloop without small boats."
*(sources: src_008)*
