# Frameworks of JoAnn E. Manson

These frameworks represent JoAnn E. Manson's structured approaches to clinical decision-making, risk stratification, and epidemiological assessment.

## HRT Clinical Decision-Making Algorithm

A step-by-step risk stratification process for evaluating a menopausal woman as a candidate for systemic hormone therapy.

**Steps**:
1. Confirm moderate-to-severe vasomotor symptoms (hot flashes/night sweats) that impair quality of life.
2. Assess age and time since menopause (ideal candidates are <60 years old and <10 years since menopause onset).
3. Check for an intact uterus to determine if progestogen is needed alongside estrogen.
4. Evaluate baseline cardiovascular, blood clot, and breast cancer risks.
5. Engage in shared decision-making to select the lowest effective dose and formulation (e.g., transdermal for metabolic syndrome), aiming for short-term duration.

> "I think we now know that some characteristics of women make them better or poorer candidates for HT, such as age, time since menopause onset, and whether the woman has other cardiovascular or breast cancer risk factors."

## Targeted Nutritional Supplementation Strategy

A precision-medicine approach to recommending dietary supplements based on individual deficits rather than universal application.

**Steps**:
1. Assess the patient's baseline diet (e.g., frequency of fish consumption, dairy intake).
2. Identify specific nutritional gaps or high-risk conditions that cannot be met through diet alone.
3. Recommend targeted supplements (e.g., Omega-3s only if consuming <1.5 servings of fish weekly).
4. Ensure supplements carry a quality control seal (like USP) to verify content and lack of contaminants.
5. Avoid mega-dosing, as excess amounts provide no additional benefit and can be harmful.

> "Additional research is needed to determine which individuals may be most likely to derive a net benefit from vitamin D supplementation."
*(sources: src_018, src_023, src_038)*

## STRAW+10 Classification Application

A framework for the clinical assessment of ovarian aging and characterizing the menopausal transition.

**Steps**:
1. Use menstrual cycle characteristics as the principal criteria for characterizing the transition.
2. Use biomarker measures (like FSH and estradiol) only as supportive criteria, recognizing their extreme intraindividual variability during perimenopause.
3. Diagnose menopause retrospectively only after 12 consecutive months of amenorrhea.

> "Because of their extreme intraindividual variability, FSH and estradiol levels are imperfect diagnostic indicators of perimenopause in menstruating women."
*(sources: src_032)*
