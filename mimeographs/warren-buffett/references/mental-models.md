This document details the mental models and heuristics Warren Buffett uses to parse reality, evaluate risk, and understand business economics.

## Voting Machine vs. Weighing Machine
A metaphor for the stock market: in the short term, it fluctuates based on popularity and sentiment (voting), but in the long term, it reflects a company's true intrinsic value and ability to generate earnings (weighing).

> In the short run, the market is a voting machine, but in the long run, it is a weighing machine.

*(sources: src_039)*

## Protective Moat
A competitive advantage or characteristic that is hard for competitors to replicate, protecting a company's long-term profits and market share.

The wider the moat, the tougher it is for a competitor to gain market share. Avoid companies whose products are indistinguishable from competitors or that rely solely on a commodity.

*(sources: src_039)*

## Circle of Competence
Knowing the boundary between what you know and are good at versus what you don't know and should avoid. Staying within this circle is more important than the size of the circle itself.

*(sources: src_036, src_039)*

## Intrinsic Value vs. Book Value
Book value is the historical cost of an asset, while intrinsic value is the discounted value of the cash that can be taken out of it over its remaining life. They are rarely the same.

Analogy: The 'book value' of a college education is its cost plus foregone earnings. Its 'intrinsic value' is the discounted excess earnings the graduate will receive over their lifetime compared to what they would have earned without it.

> Intrinsic value can be defined simply: It is the discounted value of the cash that can be taken out of a business during its remaining life.

*(sources: src_046)*

## Insurance Float
A 'collect-now, pay-later' model where an insurer holds large sums of money that will eventually be paid out in claims, acting as cost-free leverage if underwriting is profitable.

In the interim, the company can invest this money for its own benefit. If the company operates at an underwriting profit, holding this float is effectively cost-free, acting like a massive, interest-free deposit.

*(sources: src_056)*

## The Market as an Auction / Supermarket
Viewing a depressed stock market not as a cause for panic, but as a discount mechanism that benefits net buyers of businesses and stocks.

The stock market is an auction that occasionally offers pro-rata portions of outstanding businesses at huge discounts compared to the valuations those same businesses would command in negotiated corporate acquisitions.

> Overall, Berkshire and its long-term shareholders benefit from a sinking stock market much as a regular purchaser of food benefits from declining food prices.

*(sources: src_046, src_069)*

## Tailwinds vs. Headwinds
A metaphor for the underlying economic characteristics of an industry. A business with 'tailwinds' can succeed despite mistakes, while a business with 'headwinds' will struggle despite excellent management.

*(sources: src_069)*

## The Son-in-Law / Executor Test
A mental model for evaluating a potential business partner's trustworthiness by asking if you would be happy having this person marry your child or administer your will.

*(sources: src_036)*

## Key Heuristics

*   **The 10-Year Rule:** If you aren't willing to own a stock for 10 years, don't even think about owning it for 10 minutes. *(sources: src_039)*
*   **Zero Tolerance for Untrustworthiness:** At the slightest whiff of lack of trustworthiness in a potential partner or manager, walk away immediately. *(sources: src_036)*
*   **Delegate to the Point of Abdication:** Give managers extreme autonomy and stay out of their way once you trust them. *(sources: src_046)*
*   **The 5-Second Decision:** You have to be willing to hang up after 5 seconds and you have to be willing to say yes after 5 seconds when a rare opportunity presents itself. *(sources: src_021)*
*   **Eat Your Own Cooking:** Managers should have their own net worth heavily invested alongside their shareholders. *(sources: src_046)*
