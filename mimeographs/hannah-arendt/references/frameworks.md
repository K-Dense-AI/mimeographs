# Frameworks of Hannah Arendt

This document details the structured frameworks Hannah Arendt used to categorize human activity, evaluate moral judgments, and engage in critical thought.

## The Vita Activa

**Canonical statement:** A tripartite hierarchy of fundamental human activities: labor, work, and action, each corresponding to a basic condition of human existence.

**Rationale:** To understand what we are doing, we must separate activities by their fundamental nature and purpose. Conflating them leads to societal degradation, such as treating durable world-building (Work) as mere disposable consumption (Labor).

**Steps/Components:**
1.  **Labor:** Corresponds to biological necessity, metabolism, and survival (*animal laborans*). It leaves nothing durable behind.
2.  **Work:** Corresponds to the fabrication of an artificial, durable world of things (*homo faber*). It creates the physical environment we share.
3.  **Action:** Corresponds to human plurality, freedom, and the initiation of the new, occurring directly between people (*zoon politikon*). It is the highest form of human activity.

> "With the term vita activa, I propose to designate three fundamental human activities: labor, work, and action. They are fundamental because each corresponds to one of the basic conditions under which life on earth has been given to man."

## Reflective Judgment (Enlarged Mentality)

**Canonical statement:** A method for judging unprecedented events for which no established rule or customary morality exists.

**Rationale:** When traditional moral categories collapse (as in totalitarianism), we cannot rely on past rules. We must judge particulars by expanding our perspective to include the viewpoints of others, creating a valid public judgment.

**Steps/Components:**
1.  Acknowledge the particular event without subsuming it under a preconceived universal rule.
2.  Set aside purely private, egocentric concerns.
3.  Train your imagination to "go visiting" by putting yourself in the position of everybody else.
4.  Compare your judgment with the possible judgments of others to arrive at a conclusion with public validity.

> "To think with an enlarged mentality means that one trains one’s imagination to go visiting."

*(sources: src_003)*

## The Three Mental Activities

**Canonical statement:** A framework dividing mental life into three distinct, irreducible faculties: thinking, willing, and judging.

**Rationale:** The mind is not a single monolithic entity. Understanding how we process reality requires recognizing that seeking meaning (thinking), initiating action (willing), and evaluating the past (judging) operate on entirely different principles.

**Steps/Components:**
1.  **Thinking:** Deals with invisibles, tends to generalize, and pursues a purposeless quest for meaning.
2.  **Willing:** Deals with the future ('not yet') and acts as an organ of free spontaneity.
3.  **Judging:** Deals with the past ('no more') and the world of particular appearances, relying on taste and a 'silent sense'.

> "Thinking, willing, and judgment are the three basic mental activities; they cannot be derived from each other and they cannot be reduced to a common denominator."

*(sources: src_014, src_050)*

## Socratic Thinking

**Canonical statement:** A method of engaging in thought to unfreeze dogmatic habits and evaluate moral propositions.

**Rationale:** Thinking is inherently dangerous to established norms. By questioning everything, we prevent the calcification of thoughtless rules that allow evil to flourish.

**Steps/Components:**
1.  Act as a **gadfly** to arouse people from unthinking sleep.
2.  Act as a **midwife** to purge people of unexamined prejudices.
3.  Act as a **stingray** to share perplexity and paralyze the unthinking application of conventional rules.

> "Socrates gets like Midwife electric fish is not a philosopher for he teaches nothing... he only points out to them that they are not wise"
