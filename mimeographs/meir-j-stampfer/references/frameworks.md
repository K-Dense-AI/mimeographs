# Frameworks of Meir J. Stampfer

These frameworks represent the structured methodologies Meir J. Stampfer uses to measure, analyze, and interpret epidemiological data and lifestyle factors.

## 5-Factor Low-Risk Lifestyle Score
A framework for assessing adherence to a life-extending healthy lifestyle to predict mortality and disease risk.

**How to apply it:**
1. Assess diet quality (must be in the top 40% of the cohort).
2. Check smoking status (must be a never-smoker).
3. Measure physical activity (must engage in >= 30 mins/day of moderate-to-vigorous activity).
4. Evaluate alcohol intake (must be moderate).
5. Calculate BMI (must be between 18.5 and 24.9).
Sum the factors met to generate a total score from 0 to 5.

> "We defined 5 low-risk lifestyle factors as never smoking, body mass index of 18.5 to 24.9 kg/m2, ≥30 min/d of moderate to vigorous physical activity, moderate alcohol intake, and a high diet quality score..."
*(sources: src_064, src_033)*

## Isocaloric Substitution Modeling
A statistical method for analyzing the health effects of macronutrients by modeling real-world dietary swaps.

**How to apply it:**
Measure total energy intake and the percentage of energy derived from specific types of fat or protein. Include these simultaneously in a multivariate model. Interpret the coefficients as the estimated effect of substituting a specific percentage of energy from one nutrient for another, keeping total calories constant.

> "The coefficients from these models can be interpreted as the estimated effect of substituting a specific percentage of energy from fat for the same percentage of energy from carbohydrates."
*(sources: src_059)*

## Cumulative Average Method for Dietary Measurement
A longitudinal data collection framework to accurately represent long-term dietary patterns and reduce measurement error.

**How to apply it:**
Collect baseline dietary data, update it periodically (e.g., every 2-4 years), and calculate the cumulative average intake up to the start of each follow-up interval. Stop updating once a participant develops intermediate endpoints to prevent disease-induced diet changes from confounding the data.

> "In order to represent long-term dietary patterns of individual subjects as accurately as possible, we used pooled logistic regression to model the incidence of coronary disease in relation to the cumulative average fat intake..."
*(sources: src_059)*

## Bradford Hill Criteria for Nutritional Causal Inference
A framework for establishing causality in nutritional epidemiology when large-scale RCTs are practically or ethically infeasible.

**How to apply it:**
Evaluate the strength of the association, consistency across studies, temporality, biological gradient (dose-response), biological plausibility, coherence, and integrate these findings with available short-term experimental evidence.

> "In particular, in scenarios where large-scale, long-term RCTs of incident diseases are infeasible, we use the Bradford Hill criteria... to arrive at causal interpretations."
*(sources: src_045)*
