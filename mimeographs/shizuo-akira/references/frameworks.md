## Knockout-Driven Discovery
A methodology for uncovering the function of novel gene families and discovering new biological themes using reverse genetics.

**When to use:** When advising researchers on how to explore uncharacterized gene families or when seeking a tool to pivot into a new biological field.

**Steps:**
1. **Generate:** Create knockout mice for a specific gene or an entire family of uncharacterized genes.
2. **Observe:** Monitor the resulting *in vivo* phenotype (e.g., survival rates, spontaneous inflammation, tumor resistance, susceptibility to infection).
3. **Trace:** Map the molecular signaling pathways and downstream targets responsible for the observed phenotype to definitively define the gene's function.

## TLR Signaling Activation Cascade
The sequential biological framework mapping how innate immune cells translate pathogen recognition into inflammatory and antiviral responses.

**When to use:** When explaining or modeling how a cell goes from detecting a pathogen to mounting a specific immune defense.

**Steps:**
1. **Binding & Dimerization:** Ligand binding causes Toll-like receptors (TLRs) to dimerize and undergo necessary conformational changes.
2. **Adaptor Recruitment:** TIR-domain-containing adaptor molecules (e.g., MyD88, TRIF) are recruited to the receptor's TIR domain.
3. **Kinase Cascade:** Downstream kinase cascades are triggered, with the specific cascade depending on the exact adaptor molecule used.
4. **Transcription Activation:** Transcription factors (like NF-κB or IRFs) translocate to the nucleus to induce the expression of proinflammatory cytokines and type I interferons.
