This document catalogs Plato's dialectical and structural frameworks, which he uses to define concepts, test assumptions, and design harmonious systems.

## Socratic Cross-Examination (Elenchus)

**When to use:** Use this dialectical method of questioning to test definitions, expose ignorance, and clear the way for genuine inquiry.

**Steps:**
1. Ask an alleged expert a foundational question ('What is X?').
2. Elicit their proposed definition.
3. Test the definition using analogies or logical extensions to see if it is too broad, too narrow, or contradictory.
4. Expose the flaws to induce a state of perplexity (aporia), revealing their lack of true wisdom.

> "When they are confronted with their inability to explain the nature of their cherished virtue or expertise, they end up admitting their ignorance"

*(sources: src_004, src_042, src_006, src_041, src_012)*

## The Method of Collection and Division

**When to use:** Use this method for defining fundamental concepts by mapping them within a genus-species classification tree.

**Steps:**
1. Collect all instances of a generic category that share common characteristics.
2. Divide them into specific kinds based on natural differences.
3. Continue subdividing until they cannot be further subdivided, thereby 'carving reality at the joints' to find the real definition.

> "the philosopher collects all of the instances of some generic category that seem to have common characteristics, and then divides them into specific kinds until they cannot be further subdivided."

*(sources: src_006, src_037, src_041)*

## The Tripartite Class Structure

**When to use:** Use this framework for designing an ideal, just, and stable state based on natural aptitudes and the division of labor.

**Steps:**
1. Identify the natural aptitudes of citizens (reason, spirit, or appetite).
2. Assign them to the corresponding class: Rulers (Philosopher-Kings), Auxiliaries (Soldiers), or Producers (Craftsmen/Farmers).
3. Ensure each class performs only its designated function and does not interfere with the others.

> "The city is just if each of its three classes attends to its work."

*(sources: src_042, src_055)*

## The Ladder of Love (Eros)

**When to use:** Use this framework to map the progression of desire that transcends superficial physical attraction to reach the eternal Form of Beauty.

**Steps:**
1. Love a single person's physical body.
2. Recognize the commonality of beauty across all bodies.
3. Value the beauty of the soul or personality over the physical body.
4. Fall in love with broader concepts, such as laws, institutions, or the sciences.
5. Reach the top by contemplating the eternal, unchanging ideal Form of Beauty itself.

*(sources: src_028, src_037, src_006)*
