# Heuristics and Rules of Thumb

These are the pithy, day-to-day rules of thumb that Jürgen Schmidhuber applies to evaluate technology, learning systems, and the future.

## The 5-Year 10x Compute Rule
Every five years, computing power gets roughly 10 times cheaper. This predictable scaling law is the primary driver behind the success of modern AI, making computationally expensive algorithms from decades ago viable today.
> "every five years computer is getting 10 times cheaper and even when I did my diploma thesis in 1987 this was an old Trend"
> *(sources: src_005, src_012, src_013)*

## Predictability Equals Ignorability
If you can predict the next thing in a sequence, you already possess that information and can safely ignore it to focus on compressing the unpredictable.
> "if you can predict the next one it means your system already knows um this information contained in the next one and you can ignore it"
> *(sources: src_005)*

## Math Over Names
Names are not important; the only thing that counts is the underlying math. Do not get distracted by rebranding of existing algorithms.
> "Names are not important the only thing that counts is the math."
> *(sources: src_022)*

## Laziness as Intelligence
Laziness and efficiency are hallmarks of intelligence; intelligent beings naturally seek tools to minimize effort.
> "Laziness and efficiency is a hallmark of intelligence. Any intelligent being wants to minimize its efforts to achieve things."
> *(sources: src_018)*

## Seek the First Derivative of Beauty
Known beauty is boring; maximize expected fun by finding or creating data that is better compressible in some yet unknown but learnable way.
> "Interestingness is the first derivative of subjective beauty."
> *(sources: src_031, src_032)*

## Map Rewards, Don't Predict Them
Instead of predicting rewards as in standard RL, map desired rewards and time horizons directly to actions via supervised learning.
> "Don't predict rewards -- just map them to actions."
> *(sources: src_039)*

## Physical vs Virtual Difficulty
AI in the physical world is much harder than AI behind the screen in virtual worlds.
> "the real world the physical world is much more challenging than you know the World Behind the screen"
> *(sources: src_022, src_018)*

## Attribute Credit Correctly
Always try to do the right thing and correctly attribute scientific credit to the original pioneers.
> *(sources: src_023)*
