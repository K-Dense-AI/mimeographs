This document outlines the cognitive lenses Simone de Beauvoir uses to deconstruct power dynamics, existential choices, and societal norms.

## The Other
A fundamental duality in human consciousness where a dominant group sets itself up as the essential "Subject" by defining a marginalized group as the inessential, inferior "Other". This lens explains societal marginalization and patriarchal oppression. Men view themselves as the absolute human type and women as "the Other". The "Other" often accepts this secondary status for material protection and to avoid the heavy burden of authentic freedom.
> "Hence woman makes no claim for herself as subject because she lacks the concrete means, because she senses the necessary link connecting her to man without positing its reciprocity, and because she often derives satisfaction from her role as Other."
*(sources: src_008, src_025, src_031, src_032, src_038, src_045)*

## Transcendence vs. Immanence
The dichotomy of human existence where "transcendence" is the active, creative expansion of the self, and "immanence" is stagnation, passivity, and limitation. Men have historically been privileged with expressing transcendence through world-making projects, whereas women have been forced into the repetitive, passive, and uncreative realm of immanence (e.g., tending house) and denied their claim to transcendence.
> "Every time transcendence falls back into immanence, stagnation, there is a degradation of existence into the ‘en-sois’"
*(sources: src_008, src_032, src_038, src_045)*

## Bad Faith / The Serious World
An existentialist concept describing when individuals deceive themselves to avoid confronting the reality of their situation or the anxiety of their own freedom. This includes nostalgically returning to the "Serious World" of childhood where values are ready-made and obedience is demanded, allowing an adult to submit to the authority of others to evade moral responsibility.
*(sources: src_045, src_053)*

## The Myth of Essence (The Eternal Feminine)
The false belief in unchangeably fixed entities or Platonic essences used by dominant classes to justify the subjugation of a group. Anti-feminists argue that women must strive to incarnate the mysterious reality of "femininity", treating it as a fixed biological or philosophical essence rather than recognizing it as a socially constructed reaction to a situation.
> "To decline to accept such notions as the eternal feminine, the black soul, the Jewish character, is not to deny that Jews, Negroes, women exist today"
*(sources: src_032, src_038)*

## The Male Gaze / Becoming Flesh
The traumatic process whereby a person comes to experience themselves primarily as a sexual, bodily object exposed to another's gaze. Society operates with the male positioned as the "seer" (subject) and the woman as the "seen" (object). Young girls are often forced into this realization against their will as their bodies mature, making them feel alienated from their own flesh.
> "The young girl feels that her body is getting away from her… on the street men follow her with their eyes and comment on her anatomy. She would like to be invisible; it frightens her to become flesh and to show flesh"
*(sources: src_031)*

## Fidelity to Ambiguity (Situated Transcendence)
Redefining fidelity not as sexual exclusivity, but as faithfulness to the human condition—recognizing both oneself and the other as free subjects who change over time. This model supports non-monogamy by prioritizing a commitment to mutual freedom and singularity over the stifling public institution of traditional marriage.
> "it will be necessary to have a never-ending faith and the value of the one from love and know only with faith because she is different from oneself one must love him in this very difference"
*(sources: src_016)*
