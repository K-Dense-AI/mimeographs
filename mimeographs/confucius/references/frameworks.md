This document details the structured methods and frameworks Confucius uses to evaluate character, develop morality, and restore social order.

## Stages of Moral Development
The lifelong path of self-cultivation, moving from basic intention in youth to effortless moral alignment in old age.

1. At 15: Set your heart on learning.
2. At 30: Stand firm (established in character).
3. At 40: Overcome doubts.
4. At 50: Know the Mandate of Heaven.
5. At 60: Attain an obedient/receptive ear.
6. At 70: Follow your heart's desires without transgressing moral norms.

> "At fifteen my heart was set on learning; at thirty I stood firm; at forty I had no more doubts; at fifty I knew the mandate of heaven; at sixty my ear was obedient; at seventy I could follow my heart’s desire without transgressing the norm." (sources: src_000, src_034, src_033, src_023, src_046, src_037)

## Evaluating Character
A three-step observational method to understand a person's true nature and determine if they are trustworthy.

1. **See what a person does**: Observe their baseline actions and the means they employ.
2. **Mark their motives**: Understand why they take those actions.
3. **Examine in what things they rest**: Observe what brings them peace or satisfaction when unobserved.

> "See what a man does. Mark his motives. Examine in what things he rests. How can a man conceal his character?" (sources: src_034, src_033)

## Rectification of Names (Zhengming)
Ensuring language accords with the truth of things so that affairs succeed and social order is maintained.

1. Ensure the names used correspond directly with actualities.
2. Align language with the truth of things.
3. Carry out affairs to success based on truthful language.
4. Allow proprieties and music to flourish.
5. Award punishments properly so people know how to act.

> "If names be not correct, language is not in accordance with the truth of things." (sources: src_026)

## Corner-Pointing Pedagogy
An educational approach that requires the student to use their own effort and intellect to discover answers.

1. Provide initial guidance or 'point out one corner' of the problem.
2. Require the student to use their own effort to discover the remaining 'three corners'.
3. Act as a guide to impart light, rather than spoon-feeding answers.

> "point out one corner of a square... to come back with the other three" (sources: src_040)

## The Five Virtues (Cultivation of Ren)
The core practices required to develop a good moral character and create harmony in one's community.

1. **Ren**: Altruism and humanity.
2. **Yi**: Righteousness.
3. **Li**: Good conduct and ritual propriety.
4. **Zhi**: Knowledge and wisdom.
5. **Xin**: Loyalty and trustworthiness.

> "Confucianism teaches 5 virtues: Ren (Jen), that refers to altruism and humanity... Yi, that refers to righteousness... Li, that refers to good conduct... Zhi, that refers to knowledge... Xin, which means loyalty." (sources: src_037, src_030, src_023)
