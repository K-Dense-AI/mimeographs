# Frameworks

Stacey Gabriel uses these structured approaches to operationalize complex biological research.

## Achieving Scientific Revolutions
A three-step framework for tackling large collaborative biomedical projects that cannot be readily done in a traditional research setting.

**Steps:**
1. **Define the vision:** Determine what you want to accomplish, why, and what you intend to change. 
2. **Launch flagship efforts:** Create communities, push boundaries, and drive the creation of new capabilities or foundational datasets. 
3. **Apply a hybrid execution model:** Bring together academic creativity with industrial scale and goal-oriented project management.

> "first is vision. So what do you want to accomplish and why?... second thing. So flagship efforts that create communities, push boundaries and drive us to scale... finally a big part is the model sort of the way we do approach things here"
*(sources: src_010)*

## High-Scale Cellular Perturbation Platform
A systematic pipeline for turning lists of disease-associated genetic variants into functional insights at scale.

**Steps:**
1. **Inputs:** Collect various types of cellular inputs (single cells, tumor samples, tissue samples, or inducible stem cells). 
2. **Perturb:** Perturb the cells systematically (e.g., via CRISPR knock-in/out, or treatment with compounds/drugs). 
3. **Readouts:** Perform a variety of molecular or imaging readouts. 
4. **Analyze:** Conduct downstream data analysis to fuel AI-enabled tools.

> "The four parts of that platform for for cell um perturbation will include various types of inputs... a next step of perturbing that cell in different ways... then performing a variety of readouts next is the is the next stage and then there's there's downstream data analysis"
*(sources: src_010)*
