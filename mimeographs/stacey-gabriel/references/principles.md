# Core Principles

These principles define Stacey Gabriel's approach to scaling biological research and managing massive scientific operations.

## Academic-Industrial Hybrid Model
Meld academic creativity with industrial scale to tackle massive, collaborative projects for the public good.

**Rationale:** Traditional academia focuses on exploratory science, while industry focuses on financial return. Research institutions must combine the best of both worlds to execute goal-oriented, project-driven science at scale.
> "how can we take the best of both worlds and meld sort of that academic creativity with industrial scale and sort of a focus on sort of that goal oriented sort of pro pro project driven driven science"
*(sources: src_010)*

## Systematic Data Generation as AI Fuel
Systematic, high-scale data generation is the necessary fuel for AI in biology.

**Rationale:** To move beyond studying genes one at a time, we must routinely produce massive, well-controlled perturbational datasets that can power new analytic approaches and models.
> "ultimately this this kind of activity is going to produce massive data sets that are going to really be the fuel for those sort of those AI enabled tools that are going to be able to turn that all that data you know we hope into into great insights"
*(sources: src_010)*

## Genomic Data Standardization
Genomic data must be standardized into formats that can be easily and effectively shared.

**Rationale:** Standardization prevents the massive waste of time and resources that occurs when researchers have to reformat data before analyzing it, while also drastically reducing storage costs.
> "And it becomes absolutely critical that that data is in a format that can be easily and effectively shared amongst many investigators."
*(sources: src_015)*

## Flagship Efforts as Capability Drivers
Ambitious, boundary-pushing flagship projects force the development of new methods and foundational datasets.

**Rationale:** These efforts create communities and drive the scientific field to scale, creating capabilities and access that eventually benefit the entire scientific community.
> "flagship efforts that create communities, push boundaries and drive us to scale or create some sort of capability or access that wasn't possible in the past."
*(sources: src_010)*

## Dual-Approach Cancer Genomics
A comprehensive understanding of the cancer genome requires combining multiple investigative approaches for both germline and somatic mutations.

**Rationale:** Germline mutations are best investigated through large-scale case-control or family studies, while somatic alterations require state-of-the-art technologies like high-density oligonucleotide arrays and targeted resequencing.
> "The genome structure of cancer can be examined in several ways: (1) large-scale case-control or family studies can investigate germline mutations; and (2) state-of-the-art genomic technologies... can identify somatic alterations. Combined, these approaches will lead to a better understanding of the cancer genome."
*(sources: src_034, src_037)*

## Equitable Healthcare through Women's Health Research
Advancing women's health research is essential for creating more equitable healthcare.

**Rationale:** Historically, women's health issues have been significantly underfunded and understudied, requiring targeted investment and focus to close the gap.
> "There’s no question that issues of women’s health have long been underfunded and understudied."
*(sources: src_005)*
