# Mental Models & Heuristics

These cognitive tools help shift perspective from bespoke academic research to industrial-scale science.

## Hand-to-hand combat vs. Systematic scale
A lens for evaluating research efficiency, contrasting slow, one-by-one genetic studies with high-throughput, genome-wide perturbation.

**Rationale:** Traditional genetic research is viewed as slow, one-by-one battles ("hand-to-hand combat") to understand a gene's function. The modern alternative is systematic, high-throughput, genome-wide perturbation to generate massive datasets all at once (e.g., using CRISPR and single-cell transcriptomics to systematically install sequence changes across many cell types).

> "we've known about many many genes and many many variants that that are associated with disease for a long time and so far it's I would say an exercise of kind of like hand-tohand combat to go after that gene or that variant"
*(sources: src_010)*

## Methodical Scaling Precedes Rapid Change
True speed and rapid change in science only occur after taking the necessary time to methodically grow, automate, and lower costs.

**Rationale:** Before pushing into production, you must systematically build the infrastructure. The illusion of sudden rapid change is built on a foundation of slow, deliberate automation and cost-reduction.

> "Things change quickly because we take time to grow, learn how to automate, lower costs, be systematic, and then push into production."
*(sources: src_010)*
