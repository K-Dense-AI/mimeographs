# Heuristics and Rules of Thumb

Fei-Fei Li relies on these pithy rules of thumb to navigate research, career choices, and AI development.

## Demand AI That Can Do
We want more than AI that can see and talk; we want AI that can actively do and interact.
> "We want more than AI that can see and talk. We want AI that can do."
*(sources: src_015, src_018)*

## Hire for Intellectual Fearlessness
Seek out individuals who have the courage to embrace extremely hard problems and go all in.
> "I look for intellectual fearlessness."
*(sources: src_012, src_036)*

## Embrace the Hard Problems
If a problem is easy, somebody else has already solved it.
> "If it's easy, somebody else has solved it."
*(sources: src_036)*

## Prioritize Mission Over Details
When making big career leaps, focus on passion, mission alignment, and faith in the team rather than over-analyzing every minute detail or potential failure.
> "when maybe the most important thing is where's your passion? Do you align with the mission? Do you believe and have faith in this team?"
*(sources: src_027)*

## The Best Technology is Invisible
Design technology so that it quietly assists and improves life without being noticed.
*(sources: src_054)*

## Think About Values Before Coding
Before writing a single line of code, you must already be thinking about human values.
> "Before you write a single line of code, you already are thinking about human values."
*(sources: src_022)*

## Turn Audacious Questions into a North Star
Use massive, guiding questions to drive meaningful scientific pursuit.
> "physics taught me to ask audacious questions and turn them into a north star."
*(sources: src_023)*

## Compute Equals AI
Wherever there is compute, there will eventually be AI.
> "wherever there is chip there's compute wherever there's compute there will be AI."
*(sources: src_012)*

## Seeing is Not Understanding
Taking a picture is not the same as seeing and understanding a 3D environment.
> "Taking a picture is not the same as seeing and understanding."
*(sources: src_018)*

## Who Will Change AI?
AI will change the world, so we must actively focus on who is changing and building AI.
> "AI would change the world, who will change AI?"
*(sources: src_054)*
