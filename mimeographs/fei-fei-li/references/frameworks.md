# Frameworks of Fei-Fei Li

These frameworks provide structured approaches to AI development, dataset creation, and identifying high-impact research directions.

## Human-Centered AI Framework
A three-pillar approach to designing, developing, and deploying next-generation AI technologies.
1. Make AI technology more human-inspired by cross-pollinating with brain science, neuroscience, and cognitive science.
2. Rethink AI as a Humanities and Social Science field to anticipate its impact.
3. Change the verb associated with AI from 'replace' to 'augment and enhance' humans.
> "Let me lay out the three founding principles of human-centered AI framework that, at Stanford, our institute is built on..."
*(sources: src_029)*

## The Virtuous Cycle of Spatial Intelligence
A four-step loop for developing embodied AI or robotic systems that interact with the physical 3D world.
- **See:** Take in visual data.
- **Understand:** Translate 2D data into 3D spatial information.
- **Do:** Act upon the 3D space.
- **Learn:** Use the outcome to improve future perception and action.
> "teaching computers to see, learn, do and learn to see and do better."
*(sources: src_018)*

## Finding North Star AI Problems
A method for identifying the next fundamental research direction or paradigm shift in AI.
- **Step 1:** Look to evolution and brain science for inspiration.
- **Step 2:** Identify capabilities that took evolution the longest to develop (e.g., vision taking 540M years).
- **Step 3:** Target problems that are 'bordering delusional' and haven't been solved because they are fundamentally hard.
> "I ask myself, what is what evolution has done or what brain development has done."
*(sources: src_036)*

## Human-Centered AI Development Lifecycle
A framework for infusing ethical considerations and human values into every stage of AI development, from conception to deployment.
- **Step 1:** Define the problem with human values in mind (augmenting, not replacing).
- **Step 2:** Ensure data integrity, fairness, and privacy during collection.
- **Step 3:** Design safe, secure, and unbiased algorithms.
- **Step 4:** Evaluate the inference and societal impact to ensure it properly assists humans.
*(sources: src_020, src_022, src_054)*

## Dataset Hypothesis Formulation
A structured approach to building large-scale datasets to solve complex AI problems.
- **Step 1:** Define the right scientific hypothesis (e.g., visual object categorization).
- **Step 2:** Determine dimensions of quality.
- **Step 3:** Source massive amounts of data.
- **Step 4:** Implement rigorous human-filtering and quality control.
> "Every scientific quest, you have to have the right hypothesis and asking the right question."
*(sources: src_023)*

## Three Essential Capabilities of World Models
Criteria for defining, building, or evaluating spatially intelligent AI systems.
1. **Generative:** Simulate worlds following semantic instructions with physical consistency.
2. **Multimodal:** Process diverse inputs (images, video, text, actions).
3. **Interactive:** Output next states based on input actions, respecting physical laws.
> "For spatial intelligence, I define world models through three essential capabilities:"
*(sources: src_015)*

## The Golden Recipe of Modern AI
The foundational trio of technologies that unlocked modern machine learning and pattern recognition.
1. Curate massive, internet-scale datasets (Big Data).
2. Design complex neural network architectures to learn patterns from the data.
3. Utilize massive parallel computing power (GPUs) to train the models.
> "that combination of the trio technology uh big data neuronet network and GPU was kind of the golden recipe for modern AI"
*(sources: src_027)*
