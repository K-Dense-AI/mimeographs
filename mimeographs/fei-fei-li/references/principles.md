# Core Principles of Fei-Fei Li

Fei-Fei Li's principles are rooted in a deep commitment to human dignity, the evolutionary basis of intelligence, and the responsibility of technologists to shape society positively.

## AI Must Augment Humanity
AI should be designed to enhance and augment human capability, productivity, and wellbeing, rather than replacing humans or undermining human dignity.
Because AI is developed, used, and governed by people, its true value lies in extending human capabilities. We must stay vigilant about job displacement while focusing on making humans more creative, connected, and safe.
> "AI must augment human capability, not replace it."
*(sources: src_015, src_018, src_020, src_022, src_023, src_036, src_054)*

## Spatial Intelligence is the Next AI Frontier
The full potential of AI, including AGI, cannot be realized through language alone; it requires spatial intelligence to understand, navigate, and interact with the 3D physical world.
Spatial intelligence is the scaffolding of human cognition. Moving beyond 1D text to 3D world models will revolutionize robotics, healthcare, and scientific discovery.
> "To me, AGI will not be complete without spatial intelligence. And I want to solve that problem."
*(sources: src_001, src_012, src_015, src_018, src_036)*

## AI is a Civilizational Tool Reflecting Human Values
AI is a foundational technology that will reshape society, but as a tool, it possesses no independent values—it only reflects the values of its human creators.
Because AI will redefine economic, social, and cultural downstream effects, developers and governors must take active responsibility to ensure the technology improves the human condition.
> "It means recognizing AI is a tool. And tools don’t have independent values—their values are human values."
*(sources: src_020, src_023, src_026)*

## Big Data as the Foundation of Learning
Just as human and animal evolution relies on massive, continuous exposure to the world, AI requires massive, annotated datasets to learn patterns effectively.
Before ImageNet, AI models lacked the data needed to learn patterns. Recognizing that humans learn visually through massive experience inspired the push for internet-scale training data.
> "human learning as well as evolution is actually a big data learning process. Humans learn with so much experience you know constantly and evolution if you look at time animals evolve with just experiencing the world."
*(sources: src_027, src_041, src_048)*

## Perception is for Action
The evolutionary purpose of perception is not passive observation, but active interaction and movement within an environment.
Nature created a virtuous cycle where acting upon the world in 3D space allows beings to learn, and consequently, to see and do better. AI must evolve from merely seeing to actively doing.
> "Simply seeing is not enough. Seeing is for doing and learning."
*(sources: src_012, src_015, src_018)*

## Intellectual Fearlessness is Essential
True creativity and scientific breakthroughs require the courage to embrace extreme difficulty and uncertainty.
Fearlessness removes the constraints on creativity, allowing innovators to tackle hard, contrarian hypotheses where the real magic happens.
> "fearless is to be free. is to be to get rid of the shackles that constrain your creativity, your courage, and your ability to just get done."
*(sources: src_012, src_036)*

## Pursue Audacious North Star Problems
Scientists and academics should focus on massive, guiding questions that industry cannot simply brute-force with scale.
Academia should target interdisciplinary AI, theory, and fundamentally hard problems (like spatial intelligence) rather than competing with industry's compute and data advantages.
> "I would recommend you to look for those north stars that are not on a collision course of problems that industry can solve better using better compute, better data, and team science."
*(sources: src_023, src_036)*

## Guardrails Catalyze Innovation
Ethical constraints and regulations are not roadblocks; they are forcing functions that drive researchers to invent better, safer technologies.
For example, privacy requirements in healthcare AI forced the development of new differential privacy machine learning algorithms.
> "If it's done right, actually good guardrails can encourage good innovation."
*(sources: src_022)*

## Trust is Fundamentally Human
Even as AI systems become more capable and autonomous, trust operates at the human level and cannot be outsourced to machines.
Whether in backend infrastructure or consumer applications, human agency must remain the ultimate source of trust in any product or service.
> "One thing I feel very strongly is in the AI age, trust cannot be outsourced to machines. Trust is fundamentally human."
*(sources: src_012)*
