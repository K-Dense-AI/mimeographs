This document details the step-by-step frameworks Oprah Winfrey uses to navigate uncertainty and evaluate opportunities.

## The "Get Still" Decision Process
A method for making decisions when you are uncertain and tempted to crowdsource opinions.

**Rationale**: Asking others for advice usually indicates that you are disconnected from your own inner voice. The answer already exists within you, but the noise of external opinions drowns it out.

**Steps**:
1. Recognize that asking others means you don't know the answer yourself.
2. Stop asking for external opinions.
3. Do nothing.
4. Get still and wait until the answer naturally comes to you from your inner voice.

> "when you don't know what to do you do nothing you get still until you do know"
(sources: src_016)

## Intention-Based Decision Making
A filter for evaluating new projects, pitches, or business opportunities to ensure they align with your ultimate purpose.

**Rationale**: Because intention dictates the ultimate outcome of any endeavor, starting a project with a misaligned or unclear intention guarantees a poor result.

**Steps**:
1. Require the proposing party to fully think out and state their intention for the project.
2. Evaluate the proposal based strictly on that stated intention.
3. Decide if the intention aligns with how you want to impact the world.

> "Do not bring me a show, unless you have fully thought out what is your intention for doing it."
(sources: src_019)
