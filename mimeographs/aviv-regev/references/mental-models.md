# Mental Models

This document captures the metaphors and conceptual lenses Aviv Regev uses to understand complex biological systems and technological shifts.

## The Fruit Smoothie vs. Fruit Salad

**Canonical statement:** A metaphor contrasting traditional bulk genomics with single-cell genomics.

**Rationale:** Bulk analysis blends all cells together (a smoothie), losing individual cell identities and averaging out rare signals. Single-cell genomics analyzes each cell individually (a fruit salad), allowing researchers to distinguish distinct cell types and their unique genetic activation.

> "What we can do today in single-cell genomics can be thought of as the era of the fruit salad with many different kinds of fruits and each of them analyzed separately and individually and very. very rapidly."
*(sources: src_016, src_019, src_021)*

## Liberating vs. Retrofitting

**Canonical statement:** A lens for adopting new technologies by reimagining workflows rather than forcing new tools into old paradigms.

**Rationale:** Instead of forcing a new tool (like AI) into an existing, traditional workflow ('retrofitting'), one should reimagine the entire scientific process from the ground up to fully utilize the new capabilities ('liberating').

> "Think about new things in new ways. Instead of trying to retrofit a new thing into the old way of doing things say, ‘How do we liberate it?'"
*(sources: src_014)*

## Google Maps for the Human Body

**Canonical statement:** A spatial and visual framework for biological data that allows researchers to navigate across scales.

**Rationale:** Like Google Maps, a comprehensive cell atlas allows researchers to zoom out to see relationships at the tissue and organ level, and zoom in to see extremely fine molecular details of individual cells.

> "Just like in Google Maps, where you can zoom out and you see everything and you can zoom in and you can look at extremely fine detail, so would be the Human Cell Atlas."
*(sources: src_016, src_021)*

## The Periodic Table of Cells

**Canonical statement:** Viewing cells as the fundamental, categorizable base units of human biology.

**Rationale:** Just as the periodic table organizes chemical elements and allows for the prediction of undiscovered ones, a systematic classification of all cell types serves as a foundational reference tool that enables countless future biological discoveries.

> "the base unit of our body is the cell and in that sense cells are akin to other elements that we know about in science in general and in biology you can think of them similar to the elements in a periodic table"
*(sources: src_020, src_021, src_033)*

## Pointillist Sampling & Low-Dimensional Inference

**Canonical statement:** The understanding that a complete picture of a complex system can emerge from sparse but strategic sampling.

**Rationale:** Applying map-making principles to biology: just as a geographic map uses a scale rather than a 1:1 representation, a biological atlas can use statistical sampling to accurately represent a tissue without measuring every constituent cell.

> "In the same sense that when we chart the map we actually don’t do it in a one-to-one scale, we do in it a one-to-one hundred, or one-to-one thousand, the level of resolution that is needed."
*(sources: src_021, src_029, src_031)*

## The Biological Atlas as a Foundation Model

**Canonical statement:** Viewing a comprehensive dataset of human cells as the essential training data required to build computational models that 'understand' biology.

**Rationale:** A biological atlas is not merely a reference catalog; it provides the massive magnitude and scale of data needed to train foundational AI models of cellular behavior.

> "...having a map like this, an atlas as we call it, a data set of this magnitude and scale, would really allow us to build a model to understand cells. Today, we call them foundational models or foundation models."
*(sources: src_009)*

## Cells as Computer Circuits

**Canonical statement:** Viewing the complex molecular interactions within and between cells as 'circuits' or wiring diagrams.

**Rationale:** The cell's protein signaling networks process inputs (stimuli) and produce outputs (transcribing DNA), governing biological function in health and malfunction in disease. These can be systematically deciphered using computational algorithms and process algebra.

> "It’s like a complex computer that is made of these many, many different parts that are interacting with each other and telling each other what to do."
*(sources: src_000, src_001, src_021)*

## Cell Type as a Probability Distribution

**Canonical statement:** Conceptualizing a cell type mathematically as a region or probability distribution in a complex, multi-dimensional space.

**Rationale:** Instead of rigid, discrete categories, data-driven computational methods allow researchers to define cell types precisely as they go down to finer and finer biological distinctions.

> "we can think of a cell type maybe as a region or a probability distribution in some complex multi-dimensional space"
*(sources: src_020)*
