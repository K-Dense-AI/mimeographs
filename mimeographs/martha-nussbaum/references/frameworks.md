This document details Martha Nussbaum's primary frameworks. These are structured, step-by-step methodologies she uses to evaluate societal welfare, respond to injustice, and design institutions.

## The Capabilities Approach

**Canonical Statement:** A framework for measuring the quality of life and justice within a society by focusing on actual human opportunities rather than economic averages.

**Steps / Rationale:**
1. Stop relying solely on crude economic indicators like GDP, which hide inequality and ignore non-market goods.
2. Ask the core question: "What are people actually able to do and to be?"
3. Evaluate society based on central indicators (e.g., life, health, bodily integrity, education, practical reason, affiliation, recreation, environment control, emotional health).
4. Ensure political arrangements guarantee all citizens reach an adequate threshold level in each distinct capability.

> "The proposal was to measure not by income and wealth, not by utility, but rather by what people are actually able to do and to be—so capabilities or opportunities to do and be things that you value."

*(sources: src_013, src_019, src_022, src_025, src_029, src_033)*

## Transition Anger

**Canonical Statement:** A method for responding to injustice that channels the energy of anger into constructive, forward-looking reform.

**Steps / Rationale:**
1. Acknowledge the wrong and use the initial anger as a wake-up call to recognize injustice.
2. Recognize the flaws in seeking retributive payback or status-lowering of the offender (the "Payback Fallacy").
3. Reshape the retributive desire into forward-looking, constructive thought.
4. Focus on strategies that actually make sense for the future, such as rehabilitation, deterrence, and building cooperation.

> "a movement from anger, with all its defects, into forward-looking constructive thought and work."

*(sources: src_010, src_014, src_015, src_029, src_041)*

## The Capabilities Approach for Animals

**Canonical Statement:** A framework for determining the legal rights and entitlements owed to different species of nonhuman animals.

**Steps / Rationale:**
1. Consult experts who have lived closely with and studied a specific type of animal.
2. Determine what 'flourishing' entails for that specific species and individual, recognizing their horizontal variety.
3. Draft a 'legally enforceable constitution' of entitlements tailored to that kind of animal (e.g., granting dolphins the right to roam and socialize).

> "experts who have lived closely with a certain type of animal and studied those animals over long periods of time"

*(sources: src_030, src_033)*

## Liberal Education for Global Citizenship

**Canonical Statement:** A framework for designing educational curricula to prepare students for citizenship in a complex, interlocking world.

**Steps / Rationale:**
1. Cultivate the Socratic ability to examine one's own beliefs and demand reasons for them.
2. Teach students about other countries, foreign languages, and domestic minorities to build world citizenship.
3. Cultivate the imagination through arts, literature, drama, and music to help students understand what it is like to be in a position different from their own.

> "I argue that there are three abilities above all that we need to cultivate to be adequate citizens of this very interlocking and complicated world."

*(sources: src_019, src_046)*

## Metaphysically Thin Political Discourse

**Canonical Statement:** A framework for debating policy and rights in a pluralistic society with diverse religious and secular beliefs.

**Steps / Rationale:**
1. Exclude topics regarding ultimate metaphysical fates (e.g., the afterlife or the soul) that cannot reach consensus.
2. Avoid language tied to a single comprehensive religious or secular doctrine.
3. Adopt a neutral ethical language (e.g., 'human dignity') that multiple traditions can agree upon and cash out in their own ways.

> "you try to find a neutral ethical language and actually the people who frame the universal Declaration of Human Rights did a really good job on that"

*(sources: src_012, src_015)*
