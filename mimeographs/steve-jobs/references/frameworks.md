# Frameworks of Steve Jobs

These frameworks represent the structured ways Steve Jobs approached product development, organizational design, and personal life choices. They are actionable methodologies for applying his philosophy.

## The "Last Day" Test
A daily reflection tool to evaluate if your current life path aligns with your true desires.

**Steps:**
1. Look in the mirror every morning.
2. Ask yourself: 'If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?'
3. If the answer is 'no' for too many days in a row, recognize that you need to change something.

> "And whenever the answer has been no for too many days in a row, I know I need to change something."
*(sources: src_017, src_061)*

## Customer-Centric Product Vision
A strategy for developing new products by starting with the user rather than the engineering capabilities.

**Steps:**
1. Identify the incredible benefits you can give to the customer.
2. Determine where you want to take the customer.
3. Work backwards to figure out the technology required to deliver that experience.

> "It started with what incredible benefits can we give to the customer where can we take the customer not not starting with let's sit down with the engineers and and figure out what awesome technology we have."
*(sources: src_014)*

## Collaborative Product Development
A method for building complex products at scale while maintaining startup-like agility.

**Steps:**
1. Divide the project up into great, specialized teams.
2. Have all teams work concurrently on the same overarching goal.
3. Touch base frequently (e.g., weekly 3-hour leadership meetings) to ensure alignment.
4. Bring all the separate parts together into a cohesive final product.

> "We're great at figuring out how to divide things up into these great teams that we have and all work on the same thing touch bases frequently and bring it all together into a product."
*(sources: src_011)*

## Technology Windows
A timeline for evaluating the lifecycle and investment required for a new technological paradigm.

**Steps:**
1. Sense when diverse technologies are coming together to make a quantum leap possible.
2. Spend ~5 years pushing the window open by creating a commercial product.
3. Spend the next ~5 years exploiting the technology in the marketplace.
4. Begin working on the next paradigm as the current one peaks.

> "It takes around five years, or some number of years like that, to realize that window opening. And then it seems to take about another five years to really exploit it in the marketplace."
*(sources: src_033)*
