This document details the step-by-step frameworks Zhong Lin Wang uses to design self-powered systems, scale technologies, and optimize optoelectronic devices.

## Self-Powered System Design
A holistic approach to designing devices that operate independently using environmental energy.

**Steps:**
1. **Harvest:** Use a TENG to capture variable, low-quality environmental energy.
2. **Manage:** Integrate a power management system to regulate the variable frequency and amplitude.
3. **Store:** Include an energy storage unit (supercapacitors/batteries) to store pulsed energy for steady use.

> "we look for a system not only just one material is a system that's included power generator power management as energy storage"
*(sources: src_012)*

## Three-Stage Technology Roadmap
A phased approach for scaling novel hardware or energy technologies from micro to macro applications.

**Steps:**
*   **Near term (~3 years):** Target low-power, self-powered applications like security sensors.
*   **Middle term (~5 years):** Scale to medium-power applications like cell phones and large-grid sensors.
*   **Long term:** Develop major power generation (e.g., megawatts from blue energy) by solving complex durability issues.

> "We separated these into three stages: near, middle, and long term."
*(sources: src_022)*

## Piezo-phototronic Optimization
A method for maximizing the efficiency of optoelectronic devices using mechanical strain.

**Steps:**
1. Form a Schottky barrier between a metal and a semiconductor.
2. Apply physical strain to generate a piezoelectric potential.
3. Continuously adjust the barrier height using this effect.
4. Stop when the barrier reaches the optimal height for electron-hole separation.

> "we can continuous raise the barrier High by p Electric effect until you get the very best photo sensitivity"
*(sources: src_027)*

## Contact Electrocatalysis Cycle
Utilizing mechanical energy to drive chemical reactions at the liquid-solid interface.

**Steps:**
1. Bring water into contact with a material to trigger electron transfer.
2. Apply mechanical excitation (e.g., ultrasonic waves) to kick the electron back into solution.
3. Allow the electron to combine with dissolved oxygen to form reactive species.
4. Let the species dissociate target pollutants.

> "this electron can leave the surface and recombine with oxygen species dissolved in the liquid... you form a full cycle this is what happens in here we call this process called Contact electrocatalysis"
*(sources: src_013)*

## TENG Material Performance Characterization
A standardized method for evaluating materials for use in triboelectric nanogenerators.

**Steps:**
Measure the triboelectric charge density of the target material, compare it against a standard reference material (like PTFE), and use this relative charge density as the primary measuring matrix for performance.

> "Therefore, the triboelectric charge density or the relative charge density in reference to a standard material (such as polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE)) can be taken as a measuring matrix for characterizing the performance of the material for the TENG."
*(sources: src_036)*
