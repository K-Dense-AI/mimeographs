This document catalogs the mental models and heuristics Virginia M.-Y. Lee applies to evaluate scientific evidence, design rigorous experiments, and navigate her career.

## Genetic Proof of Causality (The Fat Lady Has Sung)

A lens for determining when a scientific debate is definitively settled using human genetics. The debate over whether aggregates are culprits or bystanders ends when human genetic mutations linked to the disease are identified and subsequently shown to cause the exact pathology when expressed in animal models.

> "I really think that it's not controversial and at least from my perspective, it's not controversial anymore. And I think that it's, basically what we call the fat lady had sung." (sources: src_019, src_028)

## Cell-to-Cell Transmission (Prion-like Spread)

Viewing neurodegenerative disease progression as an infectious spread where misfolded proteins act as templates. Misfolded proteins corrupt healthy endogenous proteins across connected brain regions, much like a prion, explaining the anatomical progression of symptoms.

> "it has to get out of the cell and it has to be internalized by healthy cell and then once it gets inside it would corrupt the endogenous protein" (sources: src_027)

## Conformational Strains

The concept that the specific physical conformation of a misfolded protein dictates its biological activity and pathology. Patient-derived aggregates induce unique somatic inclusions and spread differently than standard recombinant fibrils, showing that the origin and shape of the aggregate determine the disease phenotype.

> "Our work indicates that LB-derived α-Syn from diseased brains represents a distinct conformation species with unique biological activities that has not been previously observed in fully recombinant α-Syn aggregates" (sources: src_045)

## Pleiotropic Toxicity (Two-Hit Mechanism)

Viewing neurodegeneration as a dual process of toxic gain and functional loss. The cell suffers simultaneously from the toxic properties of cytoplasmic aggregates (gain of function) and the depletion of the protein from its normal functional site, such as the nucleus (loss of function).

> "In addition, the distinct possibility of pleotropic or combined effects — in which gains of toxic properties and losses of normal TDP43 functions act together — needs to be considered." (sources: src_032)

## The 'Control' Baseline

Viewing healthy, aged individuals as vital scientific baselines necessary to decode the mechanics of disease. Without a baseline of healthy aging, researchers cannot compare or identify what specifically changes in a diseased brain.

> "you need brains with no disease that's what I call a control brain without the control we would not able to compare" (sources: src_025)

## Garbage In, Garbage Out

A foundational lens for experimental rigor: flawed inputs guarantee flawed conclusions. If the initial models, patient samples, or biochemical conditions (like buffer percentages) are flawed, any resulting data or conclusions will inherently be wrong.

> "Garbage in, garbage out." (sources: src_026)

## The 'I Can' Mantra (Self-Efficacy Loop)

Using tangible, difficult achievements as undeniable proof of one's own competence to overcome imposter syndrome. Especially for women who may lack early mentorship, proving to yourself that you are capable through concrete success is the foundational breakthrough needed to sustain a career.

> "I said to myself, I can. I can go to school full time. I can run my grants. Clearly, I can sustain myself as a scientist." (sources: src_008)
