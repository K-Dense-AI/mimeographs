This document details the specific experimental and conceptual frameworks Virginia M.-Y. Lee uses to model disease, preserve tissue, and identify pathological mechanisms.

## The Cellular Pathway of Alpha-Synuclein Propagation

A step-by-step model for how Parkinson's disease pathology spreads from cell to cell.

1. **Binding:** Extracellular fibrils interact with cell membranes.
2. **Internalization:** Fibrils are delivered to lysosomes.
3. **Escape:** Fibrils resist degradation and leak into the cytosol.
4. **Seeding:** Escaped fibrils interact with soluble endogenous monomers.
5. **Maturation:** Accumulating fibrils trap cellular components to form mature Lewy bodies.

> "Failure to degrade fibrils in the lysosome leads to leakage, allowing some fibrils to escape into the cytosol. [...] Soluble monomers in the cytosol can interact with fibrils released from the lysosome... to seed the generation of more fibrils" (sources: src_044)

## In Vitro Amplification of Patient Lewy Bodies

A method for developing accurate cellular models by amplifying patient-derived aggregates.

1. Purify pathological alpha-synuclein from postmortem human brain tissue.
2. Seed recombinant monomers with a small percentage of the patient-derived material.
3. Allow the mixture to aggregate in vitro, preserving the original pathological phenotype.

> "...these LB-derived aggregates can be amplified in vitro using recombinant α-Syn to generate aggregates that maintain the unique, somatic pathological phenotype of the original material." (sources: src_045)

## Therapeutic Intervention for Protein Transmission

A framework for designing drugs to halt the progression of neurodegenerative diseases. Interventions can target three distinct stages of the transmission cycle:

1. Blocking the release of the misfolded protein from the affected cell.
2. Blocking the uptake of the bad protein into healthy cells.
3. Promoting the degradation of the misfolded proteins once inside the cell.

> "you can different have different ways to try to come up with therapy by blocking the release of this miss photo protein... you can block the uptake of the bad protein into the healthy cells as well and you can also and promote degradation" (sources: src_027)

## Brain Banking and Tissue Preservation

A meticulous process for preserving donated brain tissue to ensure it remains viable for modern molecular research.

1. Take samples across different regions.
2. Bisect the brain.
3. Slice into 1 cm thick pieces.
4. Seal in bags and freeze at minus 80 degrees Celsius.
5. Catalog all patient clinical and postmortem data alongside the samples.

> "we actually take samples on one side of the brain... bisect the brain in half and then we slap the brain this way you have now different pieces of brain they're about maybe 1 cm thick we put these pieces of brain into sealing bags... they're frozen in minus 80" (sources: src_025)

## Systematic Antibody-Based Protein Identification

A method for discovering the unknown protein building blocks of pathological accumulations.

1. Identify unusual accumulations in patient tissue.
2. Generate thousands of antibodies against the tissue.
3. Systematically use these antibodies to identify the specific building block proteins comprising the pathology.

> "we basically made thousands and thousands of antibodies so we actually systematically identify each of the protein" (sources: src_024)
